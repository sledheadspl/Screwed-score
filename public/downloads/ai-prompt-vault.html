Getting Started

How to Use This Vault

Every prompt uses [brackets] for the parts you fill in. The more specific you are in the brackets, the better the output. These prompts work with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any other LLM.

The 3-Step System: (1) Find your use case in the table of contents. (2) Copy the prompt. (3) Replace everything in [brackets] with your specific details. Run it. If the output needs refinement, add "Make it more [concise/punchy/formal/casual]" as a follow-up.

Pro Tips

  • Add "Write in my voice — here's an example of my writing: [paste 2–3 sentences]" to any prompt for better style matching
  • For long-form content, break it into sections and run one prompt per section
  • If the output is too generic, add more context: audience, product, pain point, desired outcome
  • Save outputs you love as templates — your best prompts + your best outputs become your personal system
  • Use "Give me 10 variations of the above" after any strong output to multiply your options

Part 1

Content Writing

90 PROMPTS

Blog posts, articles, newsletters, thought leadership, and long-form content. These prompts handle everything from first drafts to finishing touches.

Blog Post & Article Prompts

Blog Post OutlineWrite a detailed outline for a blog post titled "[Your Title]" targeting [your audience]. Include an intro hook, 5–7 main sections with subpoints, and a conclusion with a clear CTA. The tone should be [professional/conversational/authoritative].
Full Blog Post DraftWrite a [800/1200/2000]-word blog post on "[topic]" for [audience]. Open with a hook that addresses the pain point of [pain point]. Use short paragraphs, subheadings every 200–300 words, and end with a CTA to [desired action]. Tone: [casual/expert/direct].
Opinion / Hot Take PostWrite a contrarian take on "[common belief in your niche]." Start by acknowledging the conventional wisdom, then flip it with data, logic, or real-world examples. End with a clear position. Make it punchy and under 600 words.
How-To Post (Step by Step)Write a step-by-step guide on how to [achieve specific outcome] for [audience]. Each step should have: a clear title, 2–3 sentences of explanation, and a concrete action item. Include a short intro explaining why this matters and a summary at the end.
ListicleWrite a "[number] ways to [achieve outcome]" article for [audience]. Each item should have a bold headline, 3–4 sentences of explanation, and where relevant, a specific example or tool. Keep the total under [word count].
Case Study FormatWrite a case study about [subject/client/story]. Structure: (1) The Problem — what they faced. (2) The Approach — what was done. (3) The Results — specific outcomes with numbers. (4) Key Takeaways — what others can apply. Tone: factual, specific, story-driven.
SEO Blog PostWrite a [1500-word] blog post targeting the keyword "[target keyword]." Include the keyword naturally in the title, first paragraph, at least 2 subheadings, and the conclusion. Also naturally include related terms: [related term 1], [related term 2], [related term 3]. Prioritize readability over keyword density.
Comparison PostWrite a detailed comparison of [Option A] vs [Option B] for [audience]. Cover: pricing, features, ease of use, best for, and verdict. Be direct about which is better for which use case. Include a comparison table if helpful.

Newsletter Prompts

Newsletter Issue DraftWrite a newsletter issue for [your audience] on the topic of [topic]. Open with a personal anecdote or recent observation (2–3 sentences). Cover the main insight in 200–300 words. Add one actionable tip. Close with a question to drive replies. Subject line: [provide 3 options].
Newsletter Subject LinesWrite 15 subject line options for a newsletter issue about [topic]. Mix: curiosity gaps, specific numbers, bold statements, personal openers, and question formats. Mark the top 3 with an asterisk.
Story-Driven NewsletterWrite a story-based newsletter about [topic or lesson]. Start with a specific scene or moment (not "I want to tell you a story about..."). Build to the lesson naturally. Keep under 400 words. The takeaway should feel earned, not stated.
Curation NewsletterWrite a curated newsletter issue with the theme "[theme]." Include 5 items: each with a bold title, a 2-sentence summary of what it is and why it matters, and a placeholder for a link. Add a short intro (3 sentences) and a one-line closing. Tone: [casual/editorial/opinionated].

Thought Leadership & LinkedIn Articles

Thought Leadership PostWrite a [500-word] thought leadership piece on "[your position/belief about your industry]." Open with a bold claim. Support it with 2–3 observations from [your experience/industry trends]. End with a call to rethink the conventional approach. Tone: direct and confident.
Lessons Learned PostWrite a "lessons learned" article based on [experience/project/year]. Format: numbered list of [5–7] lessons. Each lesson has: a headline, a 3–4 sentence story or explanation, and one practical takeaway. Keep it honest — include at least one mistake.
Trend AnalysisWrite a [700-word] analysis of [trend in your industry]. Cover: what's happening, why it's happening now, what it means for [your audience], and what they should do about it. Use a confident, informed tone. Avoid jargon.

Email Marketing

80 PROMPTS

Welcome sequences, sales emails, re-engagement campaigns, and broadcast emails. These prompts are built around proven email frameworks that drive opens, clicks, and conversions.

Welcome Sequence Prompts

Welcome Email (Day 1)Write a welcome email for new subscribers to [your newsletter/list]. It should: thank them for joining, tell them exactly what to expect (frequency, topics), deliver the lead magnet with a clear link, and end with a personal question to encourage replies. Subject line: [provide 2 options]. Under 250 words.
Origin Story EmailWrite an origin story email (Day 2 of a welcome sequence) for [your brand/product]. The story should: start with the problem you faced, show the turning point, and connect to how your product/newsletter solves that same problem for the reader. Keep it under 350 words. First-person, conversational.
Social Proof EmailWrite a social proof email for a welcome sequence promoting [your product/service]. Include: one detailed success story (real or hypothetical), 2–3 short testimonials, and a CTA to [desired action]. Don't be salesy — let the results speak. Under 300 words.
Soft Pitch EmailWrite a Day 4 welcome sequence email that introduces [product/offer] without being pushy. Open with a valuable tip related to [topic]. Transition naturally into mentioning the product as a resource. End with a low-pressure CTA. Under 300 words.
Hard Pitch EmailWrite a direct sales email for [product/offer] targeting [audience]. Use the PAS framework: Problem (their pain point), Agitate (make it vivid), Solution (your offer). Include: a clear price, 3 bullet-point benefits, and one strong CTA. Under 400 words.

Broadcast & Campaign Emails

Product Launch EmailWrite a launch email for [product name] launching on [date]. Open with the problem it solves. Describe what it is in 2–3 sentences. List 4–5 benefits (not features). Add urgency (limited time/spots/price). End with a clear CTA button text. Subject line options: provide 3.
Flash Sale EmailWrite a flash sale email for [product] offering [discount]% off until [deadline]. Open with the offer immediately — no burying it. Use urgency language throughout. Keep it under 200 words. Subject line should create urgency without being spammy.
Re-engagement EmailWrite a re-engagement email for subscribers who haven't opened in 60+ days. Subject line: something that calls out the silence (e.g., "Is this thing on?"). Body: acknowledge the lapse, share something genuinely valuable, and give them a reason to stay (or an easy way to unsubscribe). Under 200 words.
Testimonial-Driven EmailWrite a broadcast email built around a single customer success story for [product/service]. Open with a compelling quote or result from a real customer. Tell their before/after story in 150 words. Connect it to what the reader could achieve. End with a CTA. Don't make up numbers.
Content Roundup EmailWrite a weekly content roundup email for [your audience] summarizing [number] pieces of content I published this week: [list titles/topics]. Give each a 2-sentence summary + a "why you should read this" line. Open with a 3-sentence personal note about the week. Close with a one-liner teaser about next week.

Subject Lines & Preview Text

Subject Line BatchWrite 20 email subject lines for [email topic]. Include at least 3 in each category: (1) curiosity/open loop, (2) specific number or stat, (3) bold statement, (4) personal/conversational, (5) question. Mark the top 5 with ★.
Preview TextWrite 10 preview text options (under 90 characters each) to pair with this subject line: "[subject line]." They should add intrigue, not repeat the subject line.

Ad Copy & Sales

75 PROMPTS

Facebook/Instagram ads, Google ads, sales page copy, landing page headlines, and offer descriptions. Built around direct-response frameworks that convert.

Facebook & Instagram Ad Prompts

Hook-First Ad CopyWrite 5 Facebook/Instagram ad variations for [product/offer] targeting [audience]. Each should open with a different hook type: (1) bold statement, (2) question, (3) "If you [situation]...", (4) number-driven claim, (5) story opener. Include a CTA for each. Keep each under 150 words.
Pain Point AdWrite a Facebook ad for [product] that opens by describing the exact feeling of [audience pain point]. Don't name the problem — describe how it feels to live with it. Transition to the solution after 2–3 sentences. End with CTA. Under 120 words.
Social Proof AdWrite a Facebook ad formatted as a testimonial/case study for [product]. Start with a before/after result (specific numbers if possible). Show the transformation in 3–4 sentences. End with "Here's how to get the same result" and a CTA. Keep under 150 words.
Retargeting AdWrite 3 retargeting ad copy variations for people who visited [landing page] but didn't convert. Address possible objections: (1) price, (2) "I'll do it later," (3) skepticism about results. Each should be under 100 words with a different angle.

Sales Page & Landing Page Copy

Hero Headline SetWrite 10 hero headline options for a sales page for [product] targeting [audience]. The product solves [main problem] and delivers [main outcome]. Mix: benefit-driven, curiosity, specific result, question, and bold claim formats.
Bullet Points (Benefits)Write 15 benefit-driven bullet points for [product]. Each should follow one of these formulas: "How to [outcome] without [objection]" / "The [adjective] way to [outcome]" / "Discover [thing] that [result]." Make each specific and outcome-focused.
FAQ SectionWrite a 10-question FAQ section for a sales page selling [product] to [audience]. Address: price objections, "will this work for me" doubt, what's included, time required, refund policy placeholder, and comparison to free alternatives. Keep answers honest and concise (2–4 sentences each).
Guarantee CopyWrite a risk-reversal/guarantee section for a sales page for [product] priced at [$X]. Make the guarantee feel specific and credible (not just "money back guaranteed"). Explain what they can expect, what they need to try, and exactly how to claim a refund. Under 120 words.

Video Scripts & Hooks

85 PROMPTS

YouTube scripts, short-form hooks, podcast intros, and video outlines. These prompts understand the difference between writing for the page and writing for the ear.

YouTube Script Prompts

Full YouTube ScriptWrite a YouTube script for a [8–12 minute] video titled "[title]" for [your channel/audience]. Structure: (1) Hook (first 30 seconds — promise the payoff), (2) Quick intro (who you are, why you're credible on this), (3) Main content in [3–5] sections, (4) CTA. Write as spoken word — contractions, short sentences, natural pauses marked with [pause].
Video Hook (30 seconds)Write 8 hook variations for a YouTube video about [topic]. Each hook should be under 30 seconds when read aloud (roughly 70–80 words). Hooks should create an open loop or promise a specific outcome. Avoid "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel."
Video OutlineWrite a detailed video outline for "[title]" targeting [audience]. Include: hook concept, 5 main talking points with sub-bullets, a pattern interrupt at the [2-minute] mark, and a CTA strategy for the end screen. Also suggest the ideal thumbnail concept in one sentence.
YouTube DescriptionWrite a YouTube video description for a video titled "[title]." Include: a keyword-rich first 2 sentences (for SEO preview), a 150-word summary of the video, timestamps placeholder section, links placeholder, and a subscribe/notification CTA. Primary keyword: [keyword].

Short-Form (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)

Short-Form Script (60 seconds)Write a 60-second short-form video script about [topic] for [TikTok/Reels/Shorts]. Hook in the first 3 words (no warm-up). Deliver one clear, specific insight. End with a hook to watch again or a comment-driving question. Write as spoken word under 150 words total.
Hook Library (Short-Form)Write 25 opening line hooks for short-form videos about [your niche]. Categorize them: (1) Curiosity gaps (5 hooks), (2) Controversy/bold take (5 hooks), (3) Relatable pain (5 hooks), (4) Surprising statistic (5 hooks), (5) Story openers (5 hooks). Each hook should be under 10 words.
Comment Bait EndingsWrite 15 video ending lines designed to drive comments for content about [niche/topic]. Each should ask a specific question or make a statement that begs a response. Avoid "let me know in the comments what you think."

Podcast & Audio

Podcast Episode IntroWrite a 60-second podcast intro script for an episode about [topic]. Open with the core problem or question the episode answers. Briefly introduce the guest or your credentials on this topic. End with a clear signal that the value is coming. Write as natural speech.
Podcast Episode OutlineWrite a podcast episode outline for "[episode title]" with guest [guest name/role]. Include: 5 key questions with follow-up probes, one segment for personal story/experience, one "hot take" question to spark debate, and a lightning round of 5 rapid questions. Target length: [30/45/60] minutes.

Strategy & Business

70 PROMPTS

Product strategy, positioning, market research, competitor analysis, and business planning. Use these when you need a thinking partner, not just a writer.

Offer PositioningAct as a direct-response copywriter. I'm selling [product] to [audience]. Here's what it does: [description]. Write 5 different positioning angles for this offer. Each angle should frame the product differently (e.g., time-saving, risk-reduction, status, simplicity, transformation). Include a headline and 2-sentence description for each angle.
Ideal Customer ProfileBuild a detailed ideal customer profile for [product/business]. Include: demographics, psychographics, top 5 frustrations related to [niche], top 5 desires, what they've already tried, where they spend time online, what language/words they use to describe their problem, and what would make them immediately trust a solution.
Competitor AnalysisI'm building [product/business] in the [niche] space. My main competitors are [Competitor 1], [Competitor 2], and [Competitor 3]. Analyze their likely positioning, pricing strategy, and target audience. Then identify 3 gaps or underserved angles I could own. Be specific and direct.
Product NamingGenerate 20 name ideas for a [type of product] that [does what] for [audience]. Names should be: easy to spell, memorable, domain-friendly if possible, and convey [desired brand feeling: e.g., speed/trust/premium/approachability]. Group by naming style: descriptive, abstract, portmanteau, metaphor.
Pricing StrategyI'm launching [product] and considering these price points: [$X], [$Y], [$Z]. My audience is [description]. My closest competitor charges [$competitor price]. Help me think through the psychology and strategy of each price point — what it signals, who it attracts, and what objections each creates. Then recommend one with reasoning.
Launch StrategyWrite a 4-week pre-launch content strategy for [product] launching on [date]. I have access to: [list your channels — email, Instagram, YouTube, etc.]. Create a week-by-week content plan with specific post/email topics for each week. The goal is to build anticipation and a waitlist of [target number] people before launch day.
FAQ GenerationI'm selling [product] to [audience]. Generate the 15 most likely questions/objections a potential customer would have before buying. For each, write a 2–3 sentence answer that's honest, direct, and confidence-building. Include at least 3 hard objections (price, skepticism, alternatives).
Revenue Model AnalysisI run [type of business] with [revenue streams]. My current monthly revenue is approximately [$X]. Analyze my revenue model and suggest 3 ways to increase revenue without significantly increasing workload. For each, estimate the effort level (Low/Medium/High) and potential monthly upside.

Social Media & Repurposing

100 PROMPTS

Platform-specific prompts for LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, and repurposing long-form content across channels. More output, less time.

LinkedIn

LinkedIn Text PostWrite a LinkedIn post about [topic] for [your role/industry]. Open with a single-sentence hook (no "I'm excited to share"). Build to a clear insight or lesson in 150–200 words. End with a question that invites professional discussion. Use line breaks every 1–2 sentences for readability. No hashtag spam — 2–3 max.
LinkedIn Story PostWrite a LinkedIn personal story post about [lesson or experience]. Structure: one-line hook → scene-setting (what was happening) → the conflict or challenge → the turn/insight → the lesson. Under 250 words. First-person. Honest. Avoid corporate language.
LinkedIn Carousel ScriptWrite a 10-slide LinkedIn carousel about [topic]. Slide 1: hook headline. Slides 2–9: one point per slide with a headline and 2-sentence explanation. Slide 10: CTA + summary. Each slide should be scannable in 5 seconds. Include a suggested cover image concept for each slide.

Twitter / X

ThreadWrite a 10-tweet thread about [topic] for [audience]. Tweet 1: hook that makes people stop scrolling (under 200 characters). Tweets 2–9: one insight per tweet, each self-contained but building on the last. Tweet 10: summary + CTA. No filler. Every tweet should be worth reading alone.
Single Tweet (Engagement)Write 10 single tweet options about [topic or opinion]. Mix formats: bold statement, question, hot take, list (3 items max), and observation. Each under 240 characters. Mark the top 3 most likely to get engagement with ★.
Twitter BioWrite 5 Twitter/X bio options for [your role/what you do]. Each under 160 characters. Include: what you do, who you help or what you talk about, and one credibility signal or personality hook. Avoid buzzwords like "passionate" or "guru."

Instagram & TikTok

Instagram CaptionWrite 5 Instagram caption options for a post about [topic/image description]. Formats: (1) storytelling, (2) tip/value, (3) question-based, (4) bold opinion, (5) behind-the-scenes. Each under 150 words. Include 5 relevant hashtag suggestions per caption.
Instagram BioWrite 5 Instagram bio options for [your account — describe what you post and who follows you]. Each must fit in 150 characters. Include a hook, what you offer, and a CTA (follow, link in bio, etc.). Make each feel human, not corporate.
TikTok Caption & HashtagsWrite 5 TikTok captions for a video about [topic]. Each should be under 100 characters, create curiosity about the video, or add context that makes the viewer want to watch. Include 6 TikTok-specific hashtag recommendations per caption (mix of large and niche).

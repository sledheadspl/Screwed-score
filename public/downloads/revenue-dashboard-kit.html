Section 01

Dashboard Architecture

The Revenue Dashboard is built as a single Google Sheets workbook with 4 interconnected tabs. Data flows in one direction: you enter raw data in Tabs 1 and 2, and Tabs 3 and 4 pull from those automatically via formulas. You never manually enter anything into Tabs 3 or 4.

4-Tab Structure Overview
Tab 1 — Income Tracker: Every dollar of revenue you earn, logged individually with date, source, type, and tax info.

Tab 2 — Expense Tracker: Every business expense, categorized and flagged for tax deductibility.

Tab 3 — Monthly P&L: Auto-generated profit and loss summary, month by month, year-to-date, with profit margins.

Tab 4 — Growth Dashboard: Month-over-month growth rates, revenue stream rankings, 12-month rolling totals, and forward projections.

Setup Instructions

1
Create a new Google Sheets file
Name it "Revenue Dashboard — [YEAR]." Create 4 tabs and name them exactly: Income, Expenses, P&L, Growth. Tab order matters for formula references.
2
Set your fiscal year
In a "Settings" cell block at the top of the P&L tab, define: Fiscal Year Start Month (cell B1), Tax Rate % (cell B2), Business Name (cell B3). All formulas will reference these cells so you can update once and it propagates everywhere.
3
Build each tab using the specs below
Follow sections 2-5 in order. Build Income first, then Expenses, then P&L (which depends on both), then Growth (which depends on P&L).
4
Freeze header rows
On each data tab, freeze Row 1 (View > Freeze > 1 Row). This keeps your headers visible while scrolling through a full year of data.

Section 04

Tab 3: Monthly P&L

This tab auto-generates from your Income and Expense data. Build the structure once — all the numbers populate automatically. It gives you a complete picture of every month's financial performance, side by side.

P&L Tab Structure

Row 1: Settings block. Rows 3 onward: months across columns (Jan, Feb, Mar... Dec), then YTD total in a final column. Rows are your income/expense categories.

Settings Block (rows 1-2, fixed reference cells): B1: Fiscal Year Start → 1 (January) or 4 (April), etc. B2: Tax Rate % → 0.28 (28% estimated rate) B3: Business Name → "Your Business Name" P&L Row Structure (Column A = Label, B-M = Jan-Dec, N = YTD): Row 5: GROSS INCOME Row 6: AdSense Revenue Row 7: Sponsorships Row 8: Digital Products Row 9: Coaching / Consulting Row 10: Affiliates Row 11: Memberships Row 12: Other Income Row 13: [blank] Row 14: TOTAL GROSS INCOME (SUM of rows 6-12 per column) Row 15: Platform Fees (negative — pulled from Expense tab) Row 16: TOTAL NET INCOME (Row 14 - Row 15) Row 17: [blank] Row 18: EXPENSES Row 19: Equipment & Hardware Row 20: Software & Subscriptions Row 21: Marketing & Advertising Row 22: Contractors Row 23: Education & Training Row 24: Home Office Row 25: Legal & Professional Row 26: Other Expenses Row 27: [blank] Row 28: TOTAL EXPENSES (SUM of rows 19-26 per column) Row 29: [blank] Row 30: NET PROFIT (Row 16 - Row 28) Row 31: PROFIT MARGIN % (Row 30 / Row 14) Row 32: TAX SET-ASIDE (Row 30 * B2) Row 33: TAKE-HOME (After Tax) (Row 30 - Row 32)

Key P&L Formulas (Column B = January)

AdSense Revenue (Row 6, Column B — January): =SUMPRODUCT((Income!$C$2:$C$1000="AdSense")*(Income!$M$2:$M$1000="January "&YEAR(TODAY())),Income!$H$2:$H$1000) Total Gross Income (Row 14, Column B): =SUM(B6:B12) Platform Fees from Expense tab (Row 15, Column B): =SUMPRODUCT((Expenses!$C$2:$C$1000="Platform Fees")*(Expenses!$K$2:$K$1000="January "&YEAR(TODAY())),Expenses!$E$2:$E$1000)*-1 Total Expenses (Row 28, Column B): =SUMPRODUCT((Expenses!$K$2:$K$1000="January "&YEAR(TODAY())),Expenses!$G$2:$G$1000)*-1 Net Profit (Row 30, Column B): =B16+B28 Profit Margin % (Row 31, Column B): =IF(B14=0,0,B30/B14) Format this cell as Percentage. YTD Total (Column N, any row): =SUM(B[ROW]:M[ROW]) Tax Set-Aside (Row 32, Column B): =IF(B30>0,B30*$B$2,0) Only calculate tax on positive profit months.
✓
Conditional formatting tip: Select Row 30 (Net Profit) across all months. Apply conditional formatting: positive values = green background, negative values = red background. At a glance you can see profitable vs. loss months for the entire year.
Section 05

Tab 4: Growth Dashboard

This tab is your strategic lens. While the P&L shows you what happened, the Growth Dashboard shows you velocity, trends, and what's working.

Growth Dashboard Sections

Section A: Month-over-Month Revenue Growth

MoM Growth % (for each month, starting Month 2): =IF(P&L!B30=0,"N/A",(P&L!C30-P&L!B30)/ABS(P&L!B30)) Format as percentage. This tells you: did you grow or shrink vs. last month, and by how much? 3-Month Rolling Average Revenue: =AVERAGE(P&L!B30,P&L!C30,P&L!D30) Recalculate per rolling window. Smooths out seasonal spikes.

Section B: Income Stream Performance Ranking

Best Performing Income Stream (by YTD total): =INDEX({"AdSense","Sponsorships","Digital Products","Coaching","Affiliates","Memberships"}, MATCH(MAX(P&L!N6,P&L!N7,P&L!N8,P&L!N9,P&L!N10,P&L!N11), (P&L!N6,P&L!N7,P&L!N8,P&L!N9,P&L!N10,P&L!N11),0)) % of Revenue per Stream: =P&L!N6/P&L!N14 (AdSense % of total — format as %) =P&L!N7/P&L!N14 (Sponsorships % of total) [Repeat for each stream]

Section C: 12-Month Rolling Chart Setup

1
Create a helper table in the Growth tab
Build a 2-column table: Column A = month label (auto-generated with TEXT formula), Column B = net profit for that month (pulled from P&L tab). This will be the chart's data range.
2
Insert a line chart
Select the 2-column helper table. Insert > Chart > Line chart. Set X-axis to month labels, Y-axis to net profit. Add a secondary Y-axis series for cumulative YTD revenue (different color).
3
Add a trendline
In chart editor, click the series > Add Trendline > Linear. This visualizes your growth trajectory and makes it easy to spot if you're trending up, flat, or down over the rolling 12 months.

Section D: Revenue Projection Formula

Simple linear projection (next month estimate): =FORECAST(MONTH(TODAY())+1, {P&L!B30,P&L!C30,P&L!D30,P&L!E30,P&L!F30,P&L!G30}, {1,2,3,4,5,6}) This fits a linear trend to your last 6 months and projects Month 7. Optimistic projection (last 3 months avg × 1.1): =AVERAGE(P&L!J30,P&L!K30,P&L!L30)*1.1 Conservative projection (last 3 months avg × 0.9): =AVERAGE(P&L!J30,P&L!K30,P&L!L30)*0.9 Annual projection (current monthly avg × 12): =AVERAGE(P&L!B30:P&L!L30)*12
Section 06

Notion Mirror Setup

Notion is your human-readable dashboard — the place you review the data visually, add context, and make decisions. Google Sheets is the calculation engine. These two work together.

Manual Sync Method (Most Reliable)

1
Create a Notion database: "Monthly Revenue Reviews"
Properties: Month (text), Gross Revenue, Net Revenue, Total Expenses, Net Profit, Profit Margin %, Best Income Stream, Notes, Action Items, Status (dropdown: In Review / Complete).
2
Create a master "Revenue Dashboard" Notion page
Embed your Monthly Revenue Reviews database in Gallery view. Pin your key metrics at the top with Notion's Callout blocks: YTD Revenue, YTD Profit, Best Month, Current Monthly Run Rate.
3
Monthly transfer (5 minutes)
On the 1st of each month, copy last month's P&L numbers from Google Sheets into a new Notion database entry. This creates a searchable, annotatable history of every month — with your own notes and decisions attached.

Automated Sync via Make (Advanced)

1
Trigger: Schedule — 1st of every month
Set a monthly time trigger in Make.
2
Action: Google Sheets — Get specific cell range
Pull the P&L values for the previous month from your P&L tab: gross revenue, net revenue, total expenses, net profit, profit margin.
3
Action: Notion — Create database page
Create a new page in your Monthly Revenue Reviews database. Map each pulled value to the correct Notion property. Set Month = previous month name, Status = "In Review."
4
Action: Gmail — Send yourself a notification
"Your [MONTH] revenue summary is ready in Notion. [LINK]" — a nudge to actually open and review it.

All Google Sheets Formulas — Master Reference

Every formula used in this system, copy-paste ready. Replace column references with your actual columns if you adjust the layout.

Section 08

Monthly Review Process

Run this review on the 1st-3rd of every month. It takes 30 minutes when your data is clean. This is the most important habit in this entire kit — the dashboard only creates value if you actually look at it.

1
Verify Data Completeness (5 min)
Scroll through Income tab: is every payment from last month logged? Check your bank statements and payment platform dashboards (PayPal, Stripe, Gumroad, YouTube Studio). Scroll through Expense tab: are all recurring subscriptions logged? Check your credit card statement line by line.
2
Read the P&L for Last Month (5 min)
Open the P&L tab. Focus on three numbers: Gross Revenue, Total Expenses, Net Profit. Compare to the previous month. Is the trend moving in the right direction? Note the Profit Margin — healthy for a solo creator business is 60-80%. Under 50% means your expenses need review.
3
Review Growth Dashboard (5 min)
Check MoM growth rate. Check which income stream produced the most revenue. Check the 12-month rolling chart — is the trend line sloping up? Note the projection for next month and set a revenue target accordingly.
4
Identify the Revenue Opportunity (5 min)
Which income stream grew the most last month? What caused it? Which stream declined or stayed flat? Is there a stream you haven't touched in 60+ days that used to perform? This is where your next month's energy should be allocated (see Section 9).
5
Transfer Tax Set-Aside (2 min)
Look at Column J (Tax Set-Aside) total for last month on the P&L tab. If you have not already been transferring per-transaction, do the lump transfer now to your tax savings account. Do not skip this step.
6
Update Notion & Write Notes (8 min)
Create a new entry in your Notion Monthly Revenue Reviews database. Paste in last month's numbers. Write 3-5 bullet points: what worked, what didn't, what you're changing next month. Set your revenue target for the current month. Mark Status = Complete.
⚠
Don't skip slow months: The review is most important on bad months. A slow month you don't analyze is a slow month that will repeat. A slow month you understand becomes a lesson that prevents the next one.
Section 09

Income Stream Optimization Guide

Your dashboard tells you what happened. This guide tells you what to do about it. Here is how to read the data and make strategic decisions about where to invest your energy.

The 4 Income Stream Quadrants

Quadrant 1: High Revenue + Growing — Double Down
If a stream is generating the most revenue AND its month-over-month trend is positive — this is your primary lever. Allocate more time, more promotion, and more optimization effort here. Do not get distracted by other streams while this one is performing. Most creators make the mistake of adding new streams before maximizing the one that's already working.
Quadrant 2: High Revenue + Declining — Defend and Diagnose
Your top earner is shrinking. This is a priority alert. Diagnose why: platform algorithm change? Market saturation? You stopped putting effort in? Competitor entered the space? For AdSense: check RPM trends in YouTube Studio. For Sponsorships: check your outreach cadence. For Products: check conversion rates and page traffic. Defend your highest earner before building anything new.
Quadrant 3: Low Revenue + Growing — Invest Selectively
A stream generating small revenue but trending upward is worth nurturing. Give it consistent attention — not all of your time, but enough to let the growth compound. Examples: an affiliate program that just started converting, a digital product with early traction, a new platform gaining momentum. Set a 90-day checkpoint: if it hasn't scaled meaningfully, deprioritize it.
Quadrant 4: Low Revenue + Declining — Cut or Ignore
An income stream generating low revenue that is also trending down is a time drain. Either make one final push to revive it (with a specific experiment and a deadline), or formally deprioritize it and stop tracking it actively. Focus is the real asset. Every stream you stop actively managing frees up bandwidth for your Quadrant 1 streams.

When to Add a New Income Stream

Only add a new income stream when your current highest-performing stream is generating consistent, predictable revenue for at least 3 consecutive months, AND you have operational capacity to develop something new without diluting your existing streams. The right time to launch a digital product is when your audience is actively asking for it AND your primary income is stable. Building on a shaky financial foundation rarely works.

Income Diversification Health Check: Healthy: Top 3 income streams each represent 20-40% of total revenue Warning: Single stream represents over 70% of total revenue (high risk) Critical: Single stream represents over 85% (one platform change = financial crisis) Target diversification for creators at scale: AdSense / Platform Ad Revenue: 15-25% Brand Deals / Sponsorships: 25-35% Digital Products: 20-30% Coaching / Consulting: 10-20% Affiliates / Memberships: 5-15%
✓
The 80/20 of income optimization: In almost every creator business, 80% of revenue comes from 20% of the income streams. Find your 20%, maximize it, and let the rest be nice supplemental income rather than your main focus. Clarity about your primary driver is worth more than a dozen half-optimized streams.

Section 01

How to Use These Templates

These templates are designed to give you a professional starting point — not a finished document. Every deal is different, every client is different, and every jurisdiction has different laws. The goal is to save you 80% of the work so you can focus on the 20% that's specific to your situation.

The Fill-in-the-Blank System

Throughout every template you'll see bracketed placeholders formatted like [CREATOR NAME] or [PAYMENT AMOUNT]. These yellow items require your input. Never send a contract with unfilled brackets — it signals sloppiness and can create ambiguity that courts may interpret against you.

👉
Before filling in any template: Copy the full text into a Google Doc or Word document. Use Find & Replace to locate every bracket. Highlight filled-in sections in a different color for your own review pass before sending.

What to Customize in Every Contract

1
Party Information
Full legal names, not usernames or handles. If you operate under an LLC or business entity, use that entity name. Include addresses — even a city/state is sufficient for most freelance agreements.
2
Scope of Work & Deliverables
Be brutally specific. "One Instagram post" is not enough — specify dimensions, format (Reel vs. static), caption length, number of hashtags, tag requirements, and story slide count. Ambiguity always costs you.
3
Payment Terms
Amount, currency, payment method (ACH, wire, PayPal, etc.), due date (Net 15, Net 30, or upon deliverable), and what happens if payment is late. Never skip the late fee clause.
4
Usage Rights & License Duration
This is where most creators get burned. Specify exactly where the brand can use your content, for how long, and whether they can use it in paid ads. "Unlimited use forever" should cost significantly more than "organic social only for 6 months."
5
Governing Law
The state (or country) whose laws govern the contract. Always try to make it your home state. This matters if you ever need to file a claim or defend yourself.

When to Involve a Lawyer

Use these templates for deals under $5,000 where the stakes are manageable. For anything above that threshold — or any deal involving exclusivity, significant IP transfers, long-term licensing, or clauses that limit your right to work with competitors — pay a lawyer to review it. A one-hour consultation typically costs $150–$400 and can save you thousands.

How to Send Contracts Professionally

The tool you use to send contracts signals your professionalism. Emailing a Google Doc link is fine for smaller deals. For anything over $1,000, consider using a proper e-signature platform.

Recommended E-Signature Tools
HelloSign (now Dropbox Sign) — $15/mo, clean UI, legally binding. Best for creators.

DocuSign — industry standard, brands recognize it, slightly more expensive ($25/mo). Use when working with large corporations who may scrutinize the signing method.

PandaDoc — free tier available, has a built-in template library. Good for starting out.

Adobe Sign — if you already pay for Adobe Creative Cloud, this is included at higher tiers.
✓
Pro tip: Always send the contract — don't wait for the brand to send theirs. Your contract protects you. Their contract protects them. Whoever sends first sets the baseline terms.
Section 02

Brand Deal / Sponsorship Agreement

This is the most important contract in your arsenal. Use it for any paid partnership where a brand is compensating you to promote their product or service on your platform(s). Modify the deliverables section heavily — it should be as specific as possible.

BRAND DEAL / SPONSORSHIP AGREEMENT

This Brand Deal and Content Sponsorship Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

Creator: [YOUR FULL LEGAL NAME OR BUSINESS ENTITY], residing at or operating from [YOUR CITY, STATE/PROVINCE, COUNTRY] ("Creator"), and

Brand: [BRAND LEGAL ENTITY NAME], a [STATE/PROVINCE] [corporation / LLC / partnership] with its principal place of business at [BRAND ADDRESS] ("Brand").

The Creator and Brand are each referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

1. SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES

1.1 Creator agrees to create and publish the following content (the "Deliverables") on the platforms specified below, subject to the terms of this Agreement:

Platform: [PLATFORM(S): e.g., YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Podcast]
Content Type: [e.g., one (1) dedicated YouTube video, minimum 8 minutes in length; two (2) Instagram Reels, minimum 30 seconds each; one (1) Instagram Story sequence of not fewer than three (3) frames]
Sponsored Segment Duration/Placement: [e.g., 60-second mid-roll read between minutes 3 and 6 of the video; 15-second integrated mention at the start of each Reel]
Required Talking Points: [LIST SPECIFIC CLAIMS, DISCOUNTS, OR MESSAGES BRAND REQUIRES]
Required Links/Codes: [e.g., affiliate link in description, promo code "CREATOR20" in caption]
Required Tags/Mentions: [e.g., tag @BrandHandle, use #BrandHashtag]

1.2 All Deliverables must include a clear and conspicuous FTC-compliant disclosure indicating the material connection between Creator and Brand (e.g., "#ad", "#sponsored", or "Paid partnership with [Brand]"). Creator bears sole responsibility for compliance with applicable FTC guidelines and platform disclosure requirements.

2. TIMELINE AND DEADLINES

2.1 Draft Submission Deadline: Creator will submit a draft of each Deliverable to Brand for review no later than [DATE].

2.2 Brand Review Period: Brand will provide written feedback or approval within [3 / 5 / 7] business days of receiving the draft. Failure to respond within this period constitutes automatic approval.

2.3 Revision Rounds: This Agreement includes [TWO (2)] rounds of revisions. Additional revision rounds beyond this number will be billed at Creator's standard revision rate of [$XXX per round].

2.4 Publication Deadline: All approved Deliverables must be published no later than [DATE], unless Brand approves a written extension.

2.5 Go-Live Notification: Creator will notify Brand by email within 24 hours of publication with a direct link to each Deliverable.

3. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 Flat Fee: Brand agrees to pay Creator a total flat fee of [$AMOUNT USD] for the Deliverables specified in Section 1 (the "Fee").

3.2 Payment Schedule: [CHOOSE ONE:]
Option A: 100% of the Fee is due within [NET 15 / NET 30] days of the publication of all Deliverables.
Option B: 50% of the Fee ([$AMOUNT]) is due upon execution of this Agreement; the remaining 50% ([$AMOUNT]) is due within [NET 15] days of publication of all Deliverables.
Option C: 100% of the Fee is due within 5 business days of execution of this Agreement (prepay).

3.3 Payment Method: All payments shall be made via [ACH / Wire Transfer / PayPal / Venmo for Business / Check] to the account or address provided by Creator separately.

3.4 Late Fees: Any payment not received within the time period specified in Section 3.2 will accrue a late fee of [1.5%] per month (or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower) on the outstanding balance, calculated from the original due date.

3.5 Taxes: Creator is solely responsible for all taxes, withholdings, and self-employment contributions on amounts received under this Agreement. Brand will issue a Form 1099-NEC (or equivalent) if required by applicable law.

4. USAGE RIGHTS AND LICENSE

4.1 License Grant: Upon receipt of full payment, Creator grants Brand a [non-exclusive / exclusive], [limited / worldwide] license to use, reproduce, and distribute the Deliverables solely for the following purposes and platforms:

Permitted Channels: [e.g., Brand's owned social media accounts (organic only); Brand's website and email newsletters]
Paid Advertising: [YES / NO] — if YES, see Section 4.2 for additional fee.
License Duration: [6 months / 12 months / in perpetuity] from the date of original publication.

4.2 Paid Advertising Usage: If Brand wishes to use any Deliverable in paid advertising (including but not limited to Meta Ads, Google Ads, TikTok Ads, or programmatic placements), Brand must obtain Creator's written consent and pay an additional Paid Ad Usage Fee of [$AMOUNT] per platform per 90-day period. This fee is separate from and in addition to the flat fee in Section 3.1.

4.3 No Alterations: Brand shall not edit, crop, alter, or modify the Deliverables in any way that materially changes Creator's message, likeness, or brand without Creator's prior written consent.

4.4 Ownership: Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of the Deliverables to Brand. Creator retains all copyright and intellectual property rights in the Deliverables. This Agreement grants only the limited license described herein.

4.5 Portfolio Rights: Creator retains the right to display the Deliverables in Creator's portfolio, media kit, and case studies for the purpose of promoting Creator's services.

5. EXCLUSIVITY

5.1 [CHOOSE ONE:]
Option A (No Exclusivity): This Agreement is non-exclusive. Creator may create content for and work with competing brands during the term of this Agreement.
Option B (Limited Exclusivity): Creator agrees not to publish sponsored content for direct competitors of Brand — defined as companies offering [DESCRIBE COMPETING PRODUCT CATEGORY] — for a period of [30 / 60 / 90] days [before / after / before and after] the publication date of each Deliverable. In consideration of this exclusivity, Brand agrees to pay Creator an additional Exclusivity Fee of [$AMOUNT], included in or in addition to the flat fee in Section 3.1.

6. KILL FEE

6.1 If Brand cancels this Agreement after Creator has commenced work on the Deliverables (defined as submission of any draft, outline, or footage to Brand), Brand agrees to pay Creator a Kill Fee as follows:

Cancellation before draft submission: [25%] of total Fee
Cancellation after draft submission but before publication: [50%] of total Fee
Cancellation after publication: [100%] of total Fee

6.2 If Creator cancels this Agreement without cause after accepting payment, Creator agrees to refund any amounts received and shall not be entitled to any additional compensation.

7. CONTENT APPROVAL AND CREATIVE CONTROL

7.1 Creator retains creative control over the format, tone, style, and delivery of the Deliverables, subject to the required talking points and brand guidelines provided by Brand in writing prior to the Effective Date.

7.2 Brand may request reasonable edits during the revision rounds specified in Section 2.3. Brand may not require Creator to make statements that are false, misleading, or that violate Creator's editorial standards.

7.3 Creator reserves the right to decline to post any Deliverable if, in Creator's good-faith judgment, posting it would materially harm Creator's reputation or violate Creator's editorial policies. In such event, the Parties will negotiate in good faith a modified approach. If no resolution is reached within 10 business days, either Party may terminate this Agreement with a Kill Fee as specified in Section 6.

8. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

8.1 Creator represents and warrants that: (a) Creator has full authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) the Deliverables will be Creator's original work and will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights; (c) Creator will comply with all applicable laws, platform policies, and FTC disclosure requirements in connection with the Deliverables; and (d) Creator does not have any existing contractual obligation that would conflict with this Agreement.

8.2 Brand represents and warrants that: (a) Brand has full authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) Brand's products and services are accurately described in all briefing materials provided to Creator; (c) any claims Brand requires Creator to make are truthful and substantiated; and (d) Brand's use of the Deliverables will comply with applicable advertising laws.

9. INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") agrees to defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any claims, liabilities, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) the Indemnifying Party's breach of this Agreement; or (b) the Indemnifying Party's negligence or willful misconduct.

9.2 Brand will indemnify Creator against any claims arising from the accuracy of Brand's product claims or the legality of Brand's products and services.

10. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

10.1 In no event shall either Party be liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including loss of profits or business opportunities, even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

10.2 Each Party's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total Fee paid or payable under this Agreement.

11. CONFIDENTIALITY

11.1 Each Party agrees to keep confidential any non-public information disclosed by the other Party in connection with this Agreement, including but not limited to campaign details, compensation amounts, and product launch timelines, for a period of [12 months] following the termination of this Agreement.

12. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [YOUR STATE], without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

12.2 Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be subject to good-faith negotiation between the Parties. If unresolved within 30 days, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules, with proceedings conducted in [YOUR CITY, STATE].

13. GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and understandings.

13.2 Amendments. This Agreement may only be modified by a written amendment signed by both Parties.

13.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

13.4 Waiver. Failure of either Party to enforce any provision shall not constitute a waiver of the right to enforce such provision in the future.

13.5 Independent Contractors. The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.

[CREATOR NAME / ENTITY]

Section 03

UGC Creator Agreement

Use this template when a brand or agency is hiring you to create User-Generated Content — video or photos they will use in their own marketing, not content you publish on your own channel. UGC deals have different IP dynamics than standard sponsorships. You're selling usage rights to content you create.

UGC CONTENT CREATION AGREEMENT

This UGC Content Creation Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE], by and between:

Creator: [YOUR FULL LEGAL NAME OR BUSINESS ENTITY] ("Creator"), and

Client: [CLIENT / BRAND LEGAL ENTITY NAME] ("Client").

1. SCOPE OF WORK

1.1 Creator agrees to create the following content assets (the "Content") for Client:

Number of Videos: [e.g., Three (3) short-form videos]
Video Length: [e.g., 30–60 seconds each]
Format/Aspect Ratio: [e.g., 9:16 vertical for TikTok/Reels/Shorts]
Number of Photos: [e.g., Ten (10) lifestyle photos]
Themes/Concepts: [e.g., unboxing, before/after, testimonial, product demo]
Brand Guidelines Provided: [YES / NO — attach if YES]
Script/Brief Provided by Client: [YES / NO — attach if YES]

1.2 Creator will script, film, and edit all Content using Creator's own equipment and in Creator's own style, unless Client has provided a detailed brief. Creator is not required to follow scripts verbatim; natural delivery is expected unless otherwise agreed.

1.3 Creator will not publish any of the Content on Creator's own social channels unless explicitly authorized by Client in writing.

2. DELIVERABLE SPECIFICATIONS AND DEADLINES

2.1 Creator will deliver all Content to Client via [Google Drive / WeTransfer / Dropbox / email] by [DATE].

2.2 All video files will be delivered in [MP4 / MOV] format, minimum [1080p] resolution.

2.3 All photo files will be delivered in [JPEG / PNG / RAW] format, minimum [2000px on the long edge].

2.4 Raw Files: [YES / NO] — Raw/unedited files [will / will not] be included. If yes, raw files are subject to the same usage license specified in Section 4 and may not be used beyond the licensed scope.

2.5 Client will provide written approval or revision requests within [5] business days of receiving Content. Silence beyond this period constitutes approval.

2.6 This Agreement includes [TWO (2)] rounds of revisions. Additional revisions are billed at [$XXX per asset per round].

3. PAYMENT

3.1 Client agrees to pay Creator a total of [$AMOUNT] for the Content specified in Section 1.

3.2 Payment Schedule: [e.g., 50% ($XXX) due upon execution; 50% ($XXX) due upon delivery and approval of all Content].

3.3 Usage-Based Pricing: The Fee in Section 3.1 covers the usage rights specified in Section 4. If Client wishes to expand usage beyond what is licensed in Section 4 (e.g., adding paid advertising rights, extending the license period, or licensing to third parties), Client must pay additional fees as follows:

Paid advertising on Meta/TikTok/Google per 90-day period: [$AMOUNT per asset]
Extended license (each additional 6-month term): [$AMOUNT per asset]
Third-party sublicense: [$AMOUNT, requires Creator written consent]

3.4 Late fees accrue at [1.5%] per month on overdue balances.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND USAGE RIGHTS

4.1 Creator retains full copyright ownership of all Content created under this Agreement.

4.2 Upon receipt of full payment, Creator grants Client a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Content as follows:

Permitted Platforms: [e.g., Client's owned social media accounts (organic posts only); Client's website; Client's email marketing]
Paid Advertising: [YES / NO]
License Duration: [6 months / 12 months / 24 months] from date of delivery
Geographic Territory: [Worldwide / United States only / specify region]

4.3 Client may not sell, sublicense, transfer, or assign the Content or any rights thereto to any third party without Creator's prior written consent and payment of the applicable third-party sublicense fee.

4.4 Client may not alter, remix, or use the Content in a context that misrepresents Creator's statements, likeness, or endorsement.

4.5 Upon expiration of the license, Client must cease all use of the Content and delete it from all digital platforms, ad accounts, and internal storage.

5. KILL CLAUSE

5.1 If Client cancels this Agreement after Creator has begun production (defined as any filming, photography, or editing work), Client owes the Kill Fee as follows:

Before any production work: No fee owed
After production begun, before delivery: [50%] of total Fee
After delivery, before approval: [75%] of total Fee
After approval: [100%] of total Fee

6. CREATOR'S REPRESENTATIONS

6.1 Creator represents that: (a) Creator owns or has licensed all elements incorporated into the Content, including music, footage, props, and locations; (b) Creator has obtained any necessary model or property releases for third parties appearing in or depicted in the Content; (c) the Content does not defame any person or entity; and (d) Creator is not bound by any exclusivity agreement that would prevent Creator from completing this Agreement.

6.2 Music Licensing: Creator will use only royalty-free, licensed, or original music in the Content, or will deliver videos without music ("music-off" versions) for Client to add licensed music. Creator is not liable for any music licensing claims arising from Client's use of Content after delivery.

7. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

Creator is an independent contractor. Client will not withhold taxes, provide benefits, or treat Creator as an employee. Creator is responsible for all applicable taxes.

8. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of [YOUR STATE].

SIGNATURES

Creator: _______________________________ Date: ____________
[CREATOR NAME]

Client: _______________________________ Date: ____________
[CLIENT NAME, TITLE, ENTITY]

Affiliate Disclosure Templates (FTC-Compliant)

The FTC requires "clear and conspicuous" disclosure of material connections — including affiliate relationships — anytime you recommend a product and stand to earn compensation. "Material connection" includes: affiliate commissions, free products, sponsored trips, equity stakes, or employment relationships. The disclosure must appear before the recommendation, not buried in footers. Here are ready-to-use templates for five contexts.

⚠
FTC Rule: Disclosures must be "clear and conspicuous" — meaning a reasonable consumer notices them without having to look for them. Burying a disclosure in the 14th sentence of a blog post does not count. Placing "#ad" in a sea of other hashtags likely does not count. When in doubt, disclose earlier and more prominently.

1. Blog / Article Disclosure

Place this at the very top of any blog post containing affiliate links, before the first recommendation.

Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links, which means I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you if you click through and make a purchase. I only recommend products and services I genuinely use and believe in. Thank you for supporting this blog.

2. YouTube Video Disclosure

State verbally within the first 30 seconds of the video AND include in the video description above the fold (before "Show More").

VERBAL (say this near the start of the video): "Quick heads-up — some links in the description are affiliate links. If you buy through them, I get a small commission at no extra cost to you. I only link things I actually use." DESCRIPTION (place above "Show More"): ⚡ AFFILIATE DISCLOSURE: Some links below are affiliate links. I may earn a commission if you purchase through them — at no additional cost to you. I only recommend products I personally use and trust.

3. Email Newsletter Disclosure

Include in any email that contains affiliate links or sponsored recommendations. Place it near the top, ideally in a distinct visual block.

📬 Disclosure: This email contains affiliate links and/or sponsored recommendations. If you purchase through my links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend tools and products I genuinely use in my own business.

4. TikTok / Instagram Disclosure

Use the platform's built-in "Paid Partnership" label when available. Additionally include in the caption:

For a sponsored post (paid): "[Brand] sent me this / sponsored this post — all opinions are genuinely my own. #ad #sponsored" For affiliate (commission-based only): "Heads up: affiliate link in bio — I earn a small commission if you buy, at no extra cost to you. #affiliate #affiliatelink" For gifted/PR (free product, no payment): "[Brand] gifted me this product. Not paid to post — just sharing because I genuinely like it. #gifted #pr"

5. Podcast Disclosure

State verbally at the beginning of any episode containing affiliate offers or sponsorships, and include in show notes.

VERBAL (say at episode start or before the ad read): "Before we get started — this episode is brought to you by [Brand], and some of the links in our show notes are affiliate links. If you use those links to purchase, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you." SHOW NOTES: DISCLOSURE: This episode contains sponsored content from [Brand] and/or affiliate links. Purchasing through our links supports the show at no additional cost to you. We only partner with brands we believe in.

DMCA Takedown Notice Template

The Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) gives you a legal mechanism to force platforms and website hosts to remove content that infringes your copyright. You don't need a lawyer to send a DMCA notice — but the notice must contain specific required elements to be legally valid. Here's how to do it correctly.

When to Use a DMCA Takedown

Use a DMCA notice when someone has copied or stolen: your videos and reposted them on YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, etc. without credit or permission; your written content (blog posts, captions, email copy); your photos, graphics, or artwork; your course content, ebooks, or paid products; your podcast episodes.

💡
Before sending a DMCA notice: Take a screenshot of the infringing content with the URL and timestamp visible. Note the date you first created the original work. If you have a registration with the US Copyright Office, have that number ready (registration strengthens your claim but is not required).

Step-by-Step DMCA Process

1
Find the Platform's Designated DMCA Agent
Every major platform has a DMCA reporting system. YouTube: youtube.com/copyright_complaint_form. Instagram/Meta: help.instagram.com (search "report copyright"). TikTok: tiktok.com/legal/report/Copyright. For websites, use the US Copyright Office's DMCA Agent registry at dmca.copyright.gov/osp.
2
Use the Platform's Built-In Reporting Tool First
Most platforms (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok) have web forms for copyright reports. These are faster than a formal written notice and often result in removal within 24–72 hours. Use the form before escalating to a formal letter.
3
If the Form Fails, Send a Formal Written Notice
If the online form doesn't work, or for website hosts, send a formal DMCA takedown letter to the platform's or host's DMCA/legal email or mailing address. Use the template below.
4
Follow Up
Platforms typically respond within 10–14 business days. If no action is taken, follow up once. If still no action, consult an attorney about additional options including a court order.
5
Counter-Notices
If the infringer files a counter-notice claiming the use was authorized or fair use, the platform may restore the content in 10–14 business days unless you file a court action. At this point, consult a lawyer.

Negotiation Guide: Terms, Red Flags & Rate Anchoring

Knowing the contract language is only half the battle. Knowing how to negotiate — what to push back on, what to hold firm on, and how to protect your rates — is what separates creators who get taken advantage of from creators who build sustainable business relationships.

What Terms Are Negotiable

Most terms in a brand's standard contract are negotiable. Brands send boilerplate knowing that most creators won't push back. Here's what you can and should negotiate:

TermWhat Brands Often Ask ForWhat You Should Push For
Usage Rights DurationPerpetual / forever6–12 months, then renewal at additional fee
Paid Ad UsageIncluded in flat feeSeparate fee, typically 30–100% of base rate per 90-day period
ExclusivityBroad category exclusivity, 6–12 monthsNarrow category, 30–60 days, additional exclusivity fee
Revision RoundsUnlimited revisions2 rounds maximum; additional rounds billed separately
Approval RightsBrand has final say on all contentBrand may provide feedback; Creator retains editorial control
Content OwnershipBrand owns all content created under the dealCreator retains ownership; Brand gets limited license only
Payment TimelineNet 60 or Net 90Net 15 or Net 30; 50% upfront for deals over $2,500
Morality ClauseBrand can terminate without pay if Creator's "reputation" suffersLimit to actions directly related to criminal conviction; remove vague language
Non-CompeteCan't work with any competitors for 12+ monthsMust be defined as direct competitors only; 30–60 days max

How to Push Back Professionally

The goal is to protect yourself without blowing up the deal. Use these scripts:

On Perpetual Usage Rights
"I noticed the contract includes perpetual usage rights — I'm happy to offer a 12-month license which is my standard. If you'd like to extend beyond that, I can offer a renewal option at $[X] per additional year. Does that work for you?"
On Adding Paid Ad Rights
"I see paid advertising usage is included in the current draft. My rate for organic usage is $[X], but paid ad usage is licensed separately at $[Y] per platform per 90 days, as the asset will be actively generating revenue for you. Happy to add that as a separate line item."
On Net 60 Payment Terms
"My standard payment terms are Net 30. I'm not able to extend to Net 60 for this engagement — but I can offer a 50/50 split with half due on signing and half due upon delivery, which keeps cash flow manageable on both sides. Would that work?"
On Unlimited Revisions
"I've removed 'unlimited revisions' and replaced it with two rounds, which is my standard. Additional rounds are $[X] each. In practice, two rounds is more than sufficient for most campaigns — I haven't gone beyond that with the last dozen brands I've worked with."
On Broad Exclusivity
"I can accommodate exclusivity for a focused time window, but the current language is quite broad — it would prevent me from working with businesses in [broad category]. I'd need that narrowed to direct competitors (companies specifically offering [product]) for a 30-day post-publication window, and I'd add an exclusivity fee of $[X] for that."

Red Flag Clauses to Watch For

⚠
Always read contracts in full before signing. The problematic language is almost never in the obvious sections — it's buried in boilerplate.
⚠
Red Flag Clauses — Walk Away or Heavily Negotiate
  • "Creator assigns all intellectual property rights to Brand" — This means they OWN your content outright. Refuse. You grant a license; you never assign copyright without a buyout premium.
  • "Brand may use Creator's name, likeness, and voice for any marketing purpose in perpetuity" — This is a blanket likeness release. Limit it to the specific deliverables and time period only.
  • "Creator may not post about competing products for 12 months following the campaign" — One campaign should not lock up your entire content calendar for a year. Push to 30–60 days, narrowly defined competitors only.
  • "Brand may modify deliverables at its sole discretion" — This lets them change what you said, put words in your mouth, or alter your message. Strike entirely.
  • "Payment is contingent upon Brand's approval of content quality" — This creates an indefinite payment hold. Replace with a defined approval window; non-response = approval.
  • "Creator is responsible for all third-party claims arising from Brand's use of content" — You are indemnifying them for how THEY use your content. This is unacceptable. Each party indemnifies for their own acts only.
  • "Brand may terminate without payment if Creator's social media engagement falls below [threshold]" — Do not accept metrics-based payment contingencies. Payment is for deliverables, not for performance you can't control.
  • Vague morality clauses ("Brand may terminate if Creator engages in conduct Brand deems harmful to its reputation") — Demand specificity. Acceptable triggers: criminal conviction, confirmed fraudulent activity. Not acceptable: brand's subjective judgment.

Rate Anchoring Tactics

Rate anchoring is the practice of establishing the first number in a negotiation to set the psychological baseline. Whoever anchors first has more control over the final outcome.

1
Always Anchor First With Your Rate
When brands ask "What are your rates?" — don't say "It depends" or ask them for their budget. State your rate first. "My integrated YouTube package starts at $3,500." You can negotiate down from there; it's very hard to negotiate up from a low anchor they set.
2
Anchor High (Aim for 20–30% above your target)
If you want $2,000, open at $2,500–$2,600. This gives you room to "meet them in the middle" at your actual target, and they feel like they got a deal.
3
Price Usage Rights Separately and Visibly
Itemize your invoice: Base content fee + Paid Ad Usage + Exclusivity Fee. This makes the premium for extra usage rights feel logical rather than arbitrary, and trains brands to understand that usage costs more.
4
The "Package Down" Approach
If a brand pushes back on price, don't just reduce the rate — reduce the scope. "I can do $2,000 instead of $2,500 if we remove the Instagram Story sequence and reduce to one revision round." This protects your per-unit rate.
5
The "What Does Your Budget Look Like?" Redirect
If you genuinely don't know where to anchor (new niche, unusual deliverable), it's okay to ask: "What budget have you allocated for this campaign?" This gives you data. If their budget is higher than your target, anchor at or above their budget. If lower, you know whether the deal is worth pursuing.
✓
Final reminder: You are not just selling content. You are selling access to your audience, your credibility, your voice, and your creative labor. Price accordingly. The creator who undercharges trains their entire client base to undervalue them.

Section 01

The 5-Stage Content Pipeline

A content pipeline is not a content calendar. A calendar tells you what to post and when. A pipeline tells you where every piece of content comes from, how it gets made, and where it ends up. These five stages run in sequence — and once you build this machine, the bottleneck shifts from "I don't know what to make" to "I can't publish fast enough."

Stage 1: Ideation

Input: Audience data, competitor analysis, trending search terms, your own experience

Output: A populated idea backlog with at least 30 validated topics

Tools: TubeBuddy / VidIQ, AnswerThePublic, SparkToro, Reddit, YouTube comments, Twitter/X search

Ideation Protocol (Weekly, 30 min)
Every Monday morning, spend 30 minutes filling your idea backlog. Use the following sources in rotation: (1) Pull top-performing comments from your last 4 posts — look for questions people are asking. (2) Search YouTube for your niche keyword, sort by "This week" — identify gaps in the top 10 results. (3) Check Reddit in your niche subreddit — find the upvoted questions with few answers. (4) Review your last analytics report — find the video/post with highest watch time and make a sequel or deep dive. (5) Check Google Trends for related rising queries in your category.
⚡
Validation rule: An idea passes ideation only if it meets 2 of these 3 criteria: (a) someone has asked this question publicly, (b) a competitor video on this topic has over 100K views, (c) there is a clear search keyword with low-competition results on page 1.

Stage 2: Creation

Input: Validated idea from backlog + content brief

Output: Raw draft (script, outline, or written piece)

Tools: Notion (briefs), Google Docs (scripts), Claude / ChatGPT (first-draft assist), Descript (audio-to-text transcription)

Content Brief Template
Every piece of content starts with a brief. Field set: Working Title | Core Question Answered | Target Viewer/Reader (1 sentence) | Hook (first 3 seconds or first line) | 3-5 Key Points | CTA | SEO Keywords | Related Pieces to Link | Estimated Length | Deadline. A brief that takes 10 minutes to write saves 2 hours of aimless creation.

Stage 3: Production

Input: Finished draft

Output: Publish-ready asset (edited video, designed graphic, formatted post)

Tools: CapCut / DaVinci Resolve / Premiere Pro, Canva, Descript, Adobe Lightroom, Riverside.fm

Production Batch Model
Never produce one piece at a time. Batch all creation in a single weekly session (3-5 hours). Record 3-4 videos back to back. Write 4-5 social posts in one sitting. Edit everything in a single editing session the next day. This reduces context-switching cost by ~60% and cuts total production time nearly in half versus producing piece-by-piece across the week.

Stage 4: Distribution

Input: Published primary piece (the "pillar")

Output: Content live across all relevant platforms, scheduled in advance

Tools: Buffer, Later, Publer, Metricool, YouTube Studio scheduler, LinkedIn native scheduler

Distribution Sequence
Publish your pillar piece first (YouTube video, blog post, or long-form LinkedIn article). Within 24 hours, distribute derivative pieces: Twitter thread goes live same day. LinkedIn post goes out the following morning. Newsletter section drops within 48 hours. Instagram Reel within 3 days. Pinterest + YouTube Community post within 5 days. This staggered cadence maximizes algorithmic surface area without flooding your audience at once.

Stage 5: Repurposing

Input: Distributed pillar piece + performance data

Output: 12 derivative pieces per pillar (see Section 2 for full breakdown)

Tools: Opus Clip (auto-clip extraction), Canva (graphics), ChatGPT (reformatting), Castmagic (transcription + repurpose)

✓
Pipeline flywheel: Once you run this system for 60 days, your idea backlog starts feeding itself. High-performing repurposed pieces generate new comments and questions — which go directly back into Stage 1 as validated ideas. The pipeline compounds.
Section 02

The 1→12 Repurposing Framework

One long-form pillar piece — a YouTube video, podcast episode, or 2,000-word blog post — contains enough raw material to generate 12 additional pieces of content. Here is the exact breakdown with formulas for each output.

⚠
Prerequisite: Your pillar piece must be at least 8 minutes (video) or 1,500 words (written) to yield enough material for all 12 derivatives without the content feeling thin or repetitive.
Output 1 & 2 — Reels / Shorts (2 clips)
Extract the two strongest 30-60 second moments from your pillar. Formula: [Provocative statement or question] + [3-sentence payoff] + [CTA to full video]. Use Opus Clip to auto-identify peak-engagement moments. Add captions with CapCut or Descript. Hook must appear in the first 1.5 seconds — either as on-screen text or spoken word. Best clips come from: the moment you counter a common belief, the moment you share a specific result or number, or the moment you tell a personal story with a clear lesson.
Output 3 — Twitter/X Thread
Formula: Tweet 1 (hook) = "I [did X / learned X / discovered X] and it changed how I [outcome]. Here's what most people get wrong: [thread]" — Tweet 2-7 = one key point per tweet, each standalone and punchy, max 2 sentences each — Tweet 8 = the contrarian insight or the hardest truth from your piece — Tweet 9 = "If you got value from this, follow me for [niche topic]. I post [frequency] about [topic]." Use numbers in your hook tweet whenever possible. Threads with a number ("7 things I learned...") outperform generic openers by 3-4x.
Output 4 — LinkedIn Post
Formula: Line 1 = your most counterintuitive finding or result (no period after — forces the "see more" click) — Lines 2-4 = context/setup — Lines 5-10 = the 3-5 lessons in plain language, each starting on a new line with a dot bullet — Final line = a genuine question to invite comments ("What's your experience with this?"). LinkedIn rewards dwell time, so aim for 150-250 words. Posts with a question at the end get 40-60% more comments on average.
Output 5 — Newsletter Section
Formula: 150-250 word summary of the pillar's main insight, written in first person with a personal angle not included in the original. Structure: [Personal hook — why this mattered to me this week] + [The core insight in 2-3 sentences] + [One actionable takeaway readers can use today] + [Link to full piece with reason to click: "If you want the full breakdown including the 8-step system, it's all here."]. Do NOT just copy-paste from the pillar. The newsletter version should feel like a private note to a friend.
Outputs 6 & 7 — Quote Cards (2 graphics)
Pull two quotable sentences from your pillar — the kind that standalone without context. Formula for selecting quotes: (a) it makes someone stop scrolling, (b) it's 20 words or fewer, (c) it challenges a belief or reveals a surprising truth. Design in Canva: dark background, your brand color accent, quote in large sans-serif bold, your handle at the bottom. Post on Instagram feed, Pinterest, and Twitter as standalone images. These consistently get saved and shared at 3-5x the rate of regular posts.
Output 8 — Blog Post / Article
Expand or slightly reframe the pillar for SEO. Formula: Use your primary keyword in the title, first 100 words, one H2, and meta description. Add 2-3 internal links to related content. Add 1-2 external links to authoritative sources. Add a FAQ section at the bottom targeting "People Also Ask" queries from Google. If your pillar was a video, get a transcript (Descript or Castmagic), clean it up, add headers, and that is your first draft. Target 1,500-2,500 words for maximum SEO indexing surface.
Output 9 — Pinterest Pin
Create a vertical graphic (1000x1500px) with a listicle-style title. Formula: "[Number] [Outcome] Tips / [Niche] Mistakes / Ways to [Result]" — use your pillar's key points as the list items. Pinterest is a search engine, so the title and description must contain your target keyword. Pins with step-by-step lists and number titles consistently outperform decorative or lifestyle pins in educational niches. Link directly to your blog post or YouTube video, not your homepage.
Output 10 — YouTube Community Post
Formula: Ask a question that relates to your pillar topic to drive engagement. "Quick question for the community — [related question from your pillar topic]. Drop your answer below — I'll share what I've learned in [day] in a new video." OR share a single stat, tip, or insight from the pillar with: "Full video dropping [day] — here's the one thing I want you to know beforehand: [insight]." Community posts boost channel visibility and warm the audience before a new video drops.
Outputs 11 & 12 — Story Slides (2 sequences)
Sequence 1 — Engagement poll: Slide 1 = provocative question (poll sticker), Slide 2 = your take, Slide 3 = swipe up / link to full piece. Sequence 2 — Micro-lesson: Slide 1 = "Stop doing X" or "Most people don't know this..." (hook), Slide 2 = setup/problem, Slide 3 = solution or insight, Slide 4 = CTA. Stories are consumed fast — use large text, minimal words per slide (max 15), and high contrast backgrounds. Add location or hashtag stickers for minor reach boosts.
✓
Total output: 1 pillar piece becomes 13 pieces of content across 7+ platforms. If you publish 2 pillar pieces per month, you have 26 pieces of derivative content — enough to maintain daily posting on 3-4 platforms simultaneously, with zero creative burnout.
Section 03

8 Automation Workflows

These workflows are built for Make (formerly Integromat) or Zapier. Each one eliminates a manual recurring task. Set up all 8 once and they run indefinitely.

Workflow 1: YouTube Video Published → Repurpose Checklist

1
Trigger: YouTube — Watch Channel for New Videos
Connect your YouTube account. Set trigger to fire when a new video is published to your channel (not unlisted or private).
2
Action: Notion — Create Database Item
Create a new page in your Repurpose Queue database. Map fields: Title = video title, URL = video link, Status = "Needs Repurposing", Published Date = today.
3
Action: Notion — Add Checklist to Page
Use a template block (or Make's text module) to append your 12-piece repurposing checklist as checkboxes inside the new Notion page.
4
Action: Gmail / Slack — Send Notification
Send yourself or your team a message: "New video published: [Title]. Repurpose checklist ready in Notion. [Link]"

Workflow 2: Blog Post Published → Social Posts Queued

1
Trigger: RSS Feed — New Item from Blog URL
Use your blog's RSS feed (yourdomain.com/feed) as the trigger. Make checks this every 15 minutes.
2
Action: OpenAI / Claude — Generate Social Captions
Pass the blog post title + first 500 characters to GPT-4 or Claude. Prompt: "Write 3 social media captions for this article — one for Twitter (under 240 chars), one for LinkedIn (150 words, professional tone), one for Instagram (casual, with 5 hashtags). Article: [title + excerpt]"
3
Action: Buffer — Create Posts
Create one post per platform using the generated captions. Set publish times to your optimal posting windows (see Section 5).
4
Action: Google Sheets — Log Entry
Add a row to your Content Log sheet: Date, Blog Title, Blog URL, Twitter Caption, LinkedIn Caption, Instagram Caption, Status = Queued.

Workflow 3: Content Calendar → Weekly Schedule Email

1
Trigger: Schedule — Every Sunday at 8:00 AM
Set a time-based trigger to fire once weekly on Sunday morning.
2
Action: Google Sheets — Get Rows for This Week
Filter your Content Calendar sheet for rows where the Publish Date column falls within the next 7 days. Pull: Title, Platform, Date, Status.
3
Action: Gmail — Send Formatted Email
Send yourself a formatted weekly brief: "This week's content schedule: [list of pieces, platform, day]. Pieces still in draft: [any with Status=Draft]. Reminders: [any with deadlines in next 48 hours]."

Workflow 4: Instagram Post → Pinterest Auto-Post

1
Trigger: Instagram Business — New Media Posted
Connect your Instagram Business account. Trigger fires when a new feed post goes live (not a Story or Reel).
2
Filter: Check Image Ratio
Add a filter: only continue if media_type = IMAGE (not VIDEO). Pinterest performs best with static images in a 2:3 or 1:1 ratio.
3
Action: Pinterest — Create Pin
Map fields: Image URL = Instagram image URL, Title = first 50 characters of Instagram caption, Description = full caption (Pinterest allows 500 chars), Board = your niche board, Link = your blog or YouTube URL.

Workflow 5: New Video Idea → Content Brief Auto-Created

1
Trigger: Notion — New Database Item Created in Idea Bank
When you add a new idea to your Notion Idea Bank with Status = "Approved," the workflow fires.
2
Action: OpenAI — Generate Content Brief
Prompt: "Create a content brief for a YouTube video titled '[idea title]'. Include: hook options (3 variants), 5 key talking points, suggested CTA, related keywords, estimated length, and thumbnail concept." Pass the idea title from Notion.
3
Action: Notion — Update Page with Brief
Write the generated brief back to the Notion page. Change Status to "Brief Ready." Assign a due date of 7 days from today.

Workflow 6: Viewer Comment → Idea Capture

1
Trigger: YouTube — New Comment on Channel
Connect YouTube. Filter: only process comments that contain one of these phrases: "can you make a video about," "what about," "have you covered," "would love to see," or contain a question mark.
2
Action: Notion — Create Item in Idea Bank
Add the comment text as the idea title. Tag it as Source = "Audience Comment." Mark Status = "To Evaluate." Include a link to the original comment.
3
Action: Google Sheets — Log the Comment
Add a row to your Audience Insights sheet: Date, Video Title, Commenter, Comment Text, Idea Captured (Yes/No).

Workflow 7: Monthly Analytics Pull → Google Sheets Report

1
Trigger: Schedule — 1st of Every Month at 6:00 AM
Time-based trigger for monthly reporting.
2
Action: YouTube Analytics — Get Monthly Report
Pull: total views, watch time (hours), subscribers gained/lost, top 5 videos by views, average view duration, impression click-through rate (CTR), revenue (if monetized).
3
Action: Google Sheets — Append Monthly Data Row
Write all pulled metrics to a new row in your Analytics History sheet. This builds your longitudinal data set automatically with zero manual entry.
4
Action: Gmail — Send Monthly Summary
Send yourself a formatted summary of the pulled data for a quick monthly pulse check.

Workflow 8: Content Gap Analysis → Weekly Topic Suggestions

1
Trigger: Schedule — Every Friday at 9:00 AM
Weekly trigger to run your gap analysis every end-of-week.
2
Action: Google Sheets — Read Your Content Log
Pull all published content from your Content Log. Extract the topics and keywords already covered.
3
Action: OpenAI — Generate Gap Analysis
Prompt: "I create content about [your niche]. Here are the topics I've already covered: [list]. Based on common questions in this niche, suggest 5 topics I have NOT yet covered that have high audience demand. Format as a numbered list with a 1-sentence rationale for each."
4
Action: Notion — Add 5 Ideas to Idea Bank
Create 5 new items in your Notion Idea Bank, one per suggested topic. Status = "To Evaluate," Source = "AI Gap Analysis."

Section 05

Platform-Specific Best Practices

YouTube

Format Specs
Thumbnail: 1280x720px, JPG or PNG, under 2MB. Video: 1920x1080 minimum (4K preferred), MP4, H.264. Title: 60 characters max. Description: First 150 characters visible before "Show More" — put your CTA and keyword here. Tags: 5-8 highly relevant tags, not 30 generic ones.
Best Posting Times
Friday 12PM-3PM EST or Saturday 9AM-12PM EST for general audiences. Business/creator niches skew toward Tuesday and Thursday 8AM-10AM. Upload 2-4 hours before your target peak window — YouTube takes time to index and begin distribution.
10 Thumbnail Hook Formulas
1. The Number Promise: "17 Tools That Changed My Business" — number + bold color contrast
2. The Mistake Frame: "I Was Wrong About [Topic]" — your surprised face + bold text
3. The Before/After Split: Two side-by-side images showing transformation
4. The Curiosity Gap: "Why Everyone Is Wrong About [X]" — cryptic enough to need the click
5. The Tutorial Proof: Screenshot of result + "How I Did This"
6. The Comparison: "X vs Y — Which Is Better?" — two opposing visuals
7. The Story Setup: Your face looking shocked/excited + "What Happened When I..."
8. The Income Reveal: Dollar amount or metric prominently displayed
9. The Tool/Hack: Product or interface screenshot + "This Changed Everything"
10. The Warning: Red warning icon + "Stop Doing X" — high urgency, high CTR

Instagram

Format Specs & Ratios
Feed square: 1080x1080px. Feed portrait (recommended): 1080x1350px. Reel: 1080x1920px. Story: 1080x1920px. Carousel: up to 10 slides, any aspect ratio (portrait recommended). Keep safe zone: stay 150px from all edges on Stories and Reels to avoid UI overlay.
Grid Strategy: 3-Post Color Patterns
Plan your grid in rows of 3. Each row should follow a consistent tone: Row pattern A = dark-dark-light (creates visual rhythm). Row pattern B = brand color-neutral-brand color (alternating pops). Maintain your brand palette across all feed posts. Profiles with a cohesive grid convert browsers to followers at 2-3x the rate of inconsistent grids.
Best Posting Times
Monday, Wednesday, Friday: 9AM-11AM and 7PM-9PM local time. Reels: Tuesday and Thursday 12PM-2PM consistently outperform other windows. Stories: 8AM-10AM for weekday morning reach. Always post Reels at the beginning of the week — the algorithm prioritizes new Reels for 24-48 hours post-upload.

LinkedIn

Format Specs
Profile banner: 1584x396px. Post images: 1200x627px (landscape) or 1080x1080px (square). Carousel PDFs: 1080x1080px slides, max 300 pages, PDF format. Document posts (carousels) currently get the highest organic reach of any format on LinkedIn.
Best Posting Times
Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday between 8AM-10AM or 12PM-1PM in your target audience's timezone. LinkedIn engagement drops sharply on weekends — only post Friday if you need the extra slot. Never post after 5PM on weekdays.
Hook Formula for LinkedIn
The LinkedIn algorithm cuts text after 3 lines. Your first 3 lines must be a complete hook. Formula: [Bold declarative statement or contrarian claim]. [One sentence that creates tension or curiosity]. [Promise of what follows: "Here's what I've learned:"] — Never start with "I" as your first word. Never start with a question. Lead with your insight, not your setup.

TikTok

Format Specs
Video: 1080x1920px (9:16). Length: 15-60 seconds performs best for non-educational content. Educational: 60-3 minutes. Caption: 150 characters visible, 2,200 max. Use 3-5 hashtags — one broad (#marketing), one niche (#creatortips), one trending. Do NOT stuff hashtags — TikTok's algorithm deprioritizes spammy tag usage.
Best Posting Times
6AM-10AM EST (catches morning scroll) and 7PM-10PM EST (prime evening usage). Post 1-2x per day for fastest growth. TikTok is the only platform where daily posting consistently outperforms 3x/week even for accounts with small audiences.

Twitter / X

Format Specs
Header image: 1500x500px. Post images: 1200x675px (16:9) or 1080x1080px (square). Thread: up to 25 tweets per thread (optimal: 7-12 tweets). Images attached to tweets get 2-3x more impressions than text-only posts.
Best Posting Times
Monday-Friday, 8AM-10AM and 12PM-1PM EST. Threads launched on weekday mornings outperform evening launches by 40-60% in engagement. Avoid posting after 8PM — your content will be buried by morning. The "golden window" for threads is Tuesday 9AM EST.
Thread Formatting Guide
Tweet 1: Hook (must work standalone). Number your tweets at the start (1/, 2/). Use line breaks — never write walls of text. End on a strong insight, not a fizzle. The last tweet should be the most quotable. Reply to your own thread with a summary after it gains traction to extend reach.

Weekly Check-In Process (15 minutes every Monday)

1
Pull Last Week's Numbers
Open your analytics dashboard (or Metricool for cross-platform). Note the primary metric for each platform. Record in your Google Sheets Analytics History tab.
2
Identify the Week's Winner
Which single piece of content had the highest reach or engagement relative to expectations? Note the topic, format, platform, and hook. This informs what to replicate.
3
Identify the Week's Underperformer
Which piece fell flat? Diagnose: was it the hook, the topic, the format, the platform, or the timing? One-word diagnosis goes in your Notes column.
4
Set This Week's Experiment
Based on last week's winner, set one deliberate experiment for this week: a new thumbnail style, a different hook format, a posting time shift, or a new content type.
5
Update Your Content Calendar
Confirm all content for the coming week is in "Scheduled" or "In Production" status. No piece should still be in "Idea" status if it publishes within 72 hours.
⚠
The 30-day rule: Do not make platform or format decisions based on fewer than 30 days of data. A single bad week means nothing. A 4-week trend is actionable signal. Most creators abandon formats too early because they mistake a bad week for a bad strategy.

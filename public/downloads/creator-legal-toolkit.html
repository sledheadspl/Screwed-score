Influencer / Creator Agreement

Creator & Brand / Sponsor

How to Use This Template

  • Replace every [PLACEHOLDER] with your actual information before sending to any party.
  • Specify deliverables as precisely as possible — video length, posting platform, number of stories, format (Reel vs. static post), etc.
  • FTC disclosure requirements are non-negotiable in the U.S. — do not remove Section 8. Review current FTC guidelines at ftc.gov/influencers.
  • If the brand requests exclusivity, negotiate compensation accordingly — exclusivity windows should be time-limited and clearly defined.
  • Both parties should sign before any work begins. Verbal agreements and DMs are not enforceable substitutes for a written contract.
  • Consider having a lawyer review this for deals over $5,000.
Influencer / Creator Agreement
Sponsored Content & Campaign Agreement

This Influencer / Creator Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] (the "Effective Date") by and between:

Brand / Sponsor: [BRAND LEGAL NAME], a [STATE/PROVINCE] [ENTITY TYPE, e.g., LLC / Corporation], with its principal place of business at [BRAND ADDRESS] (the "Brand"); and

Creator: [CREATOR FULL LEGAL NAME], an individual / [ENTITY TYPE IF APPLICABLE] residing or operating at [CREATOR ADDRESS], known online as [HANDLE / CHANNEL NAME] (the "Creator").

Brand and Creator are each referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

1. Campaign Description

The Brand is engaging the Creator to produce and publish sponsored content for the following campaign: [CAMPAIGN NAME / PRODUCT BEING PROMOTED] (the "Campaign"). The purpose of the Campaign is to [DESCRIBE CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE, e.g., promote the launch of Brand's new skincare line to Creator's audience]. The Campaign shall run from [START DATE] through [END DATE] (the "Campaign Period").

2. Deliverables

Creator agrees to produce and publish the following content (collectively, the "Deliverables") on the platforms and within the timeframes specified:

  • [NUMBER] sponsored video(s) of at least [LENGTH, e.g., 60 seconds] each, posted on [PLATFORM, e.g., YouTube] by [DATE].
  • [NUMBER] sponsored Instagram post(s) (static image or Reel) posted on [PLATFORM] by [DATE].
  • [NUMBER] Instagram / TikTok Stories posted by [DATE], including a swipe-up or link-in-bio call to action.
  • [ANY ADDITIONAL DELIVERABLES, e.g., blog post, newsletter mention, podcast ad read].

All Deliverables must remain live and publicly accessible (not deleted, privatized, or unlisted) for a minimum of [e.g., 12 months] from the date of posting unless otherwise agreed in writing.

3. Content Approval Process

Creator shall submit all Deliverables for Brand review at least [e.g., 5] business days prior to the agreed posting date. Brand shall provide written approval, revision requests, or rejection within [e.g., 3] business days of receipt. Brand is limited to [e.g., two (2)] rounds of revision requests per Deliverable. If Brand does not respond within the specified review window, the content shall be deemed approved. Revisions shall not materially alter the Creator's authentic voice or editorial style without Creator's written consent.

4. Compensation

In consideration for the Deliverables and rights granted herein, Brand shall pay Creator a flat fee of [$AMOUNT USD] (the "Fee"), payable as follows:

  • Deposit: [e.g., 50% ($AMOUNT)] due upon execution of this Agreement.
  • Balance: [e.g., remaining 50% ($AMOUNT)] due within [e.g., 14] days of Brand's approval of all Deliverables.

Payment shall be made via [PAYMENT METHOD, e.g., bank transfer / PayPal / check] to [CREATOR PAYMENT DETAILS]. In addition to the flat fee, Brand shall provide Creator with [PRODUCT / GIFTING TERMS, e.g., one (1) complimentary product package valued at approximately $X, or "N/A"]. All compensation received under this Agreement is subject to applicable taxes. Creator is responsible for all income taxes on amounts received. Creator is an independent contractor and not an employee of Brand.

5. Timeline

The key project milestones are as follows:

  • [DATE] — Agreement signed by both Parties.
  • [DATE] — Brand delivers product samples / creative brief / talking points to Creator.
  • [DATE] — Creator submits draft content for Brand review.
  • [DATE] — Brand provides feedback.
  • [DATE] — Final content approved and posted.
  • [DATE] — Final payment due.

6. Exclusivity

During the Campaign Period and for [e.g., 30 / 60 / 90] days thereafter (the "Exclusivity Window"), Creator agrees not to produce sponsored content for, or accept payment from, any direct competitor of Brand. For purposes of this Agreement, "direct competitor" means any company offering [DESCRIBE COMPETING PRODUCT/SERVICE CATEGORY, e.g., direct-to-consumer skincare products]. This exclusivity clause does not restrict Creator from discussing or reviewing non-sponsored products or from engaging in any non-competing business activities. If no exclusivity is required, this Section shall be marked "N/A" and shall have no force or effect.

7. Usage Rights & License

Creator grants Brand a [exclusive / non-exclusive], worldwide, royalty-free license to repost, share, and use the Deliverables for Brand's own social media channels, website, and internal purposes for a period of [e.g., 12 months] following publication. This license does not include: (a) paid advertising or "whitelisting" / "dark posting" without separate written consent and additional compensation; (b) modification of the Deliverables in a manner that misrepresents Creator's opinion; or (c) use in any context that would damage Creator's reputation. Creator retains all underlying copyright in the Deliverables. If Brand requires expanded rights (e.g., for paid media), the Parties shall negotiate a separate usage fee.

8. FTC Disclosure Requirements

Creator acknowledges that the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) requires clear and conspicuous disclosure of material connections between creators and brands. Creator agrees to comply with all applicable FTC guidelines and any platform-specific disclosure requirements. At minimum, Creator shall include in all Deliverables a clear disclosure such as #ad, #sponsored, or #paidpartnership (as appropriate to the platform), placed prominently and before any "more" or "see more" truncation. Creator shall not make any false or misleading claims about Brand's products or services. Failure to include required disclosures is a material breach of this Agreement and Creator assumes full liability for any resulting regulatory action.

9. Creator Representations & Warranties

Creator represents and warrants that: (a) Creator has the full right and authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) the Deliverables will be Creator's original work and will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights; (c) Creator's platforms are in good standing and have not been subject to bans, suspensions, or significant follower manipulation; (d) Creator has disclosed any existing exclusivity or non-compete obligations that may affect this Agreement; and (e) Creator will comply with all applicable laws in creating and publishing the Deliverables.

10. Brand Representations & Warranties

Brand represents and warrants that: (a) Brand has the full right and authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) all products, services, and information provided to Creator are accurately described and comply with applicable law; (c) the use of Creator's content as permitted herein will not infringe any third-party rights; and (d) Brand will pay Creator in accordance with Section 4.

11. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement with [e.g., 7] days' written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within the notice period. Brand may terminate for convenience upon written notice, provided Brand pays Creator a kill fee equal to [e.g., 50%] of the Fee for any unposted Deliverables, plus full payment for any Deliverables already posted. Creator may terminate if Brand fails to make any payment when due and does not cure within [e.g., 5] business days of notice. Upon termination, any license granted to Brand under Section 7 shall terminate immediately as to unposted Deliverables; previously posted content may remain live unless otherwise agreed.

12. Confidentiality

Each Party agrees to keep the terms of this Agreement and any non-public information shared by the other Party confidential and to not disclose such information to third parties without prior written consent, except as required by law or to the Party's legal and financial advisors bound by confidentiality obligations.

13. Indemnification

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, losses, liabilities, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from: (a) the Indemnifying Party's breach of any representation, warranty, or obligation under this Agreement; or (b) the Indemnifying Party's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

14. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [STATE], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any dispute arising out of or related to this Agreement shall first be subject to good-faith negotiation between the Parties. If unresolved within [e.g., 30] days, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration in [CITY, STATE] under the rules of the American Arbitration Association, with the costs split equally unless the arbitrator awards otherwise. The prevailing Party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees.

15. General Provisions

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings. This Agreement may not be amended except in a signed writing. If any provision is found unenforceable, the remainder shall continue in full force. Neither Party may assign this Agreement without prior written consent. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, and electronic signatures shall be deemed valid.

02

Brand Partnership Contract

Long-Term Ambassador Relationship

How to Use This Template

  • This template is designed for ongoing ambassador relationships (3–12+ months), not one-off sponsored posts. Use Contract 1 for single campaigns.
  • Define exclusivity categories precisely — broad exclusivity dramatically limits your earning potential and should command premium pay.
  • Morality clauses work both ways. Consider adding protections against Brand conduct that could harm your reputation.
  • Performance bonuses should be tied to specific, measurable metrics (sales attributed to your promo code, link clicks, etc.).
  • Negotiate content calendar terms upfront — brands often try to add deliverables mid-partnership without additional compensation.
  • Ensure exit clause terms are mutual so both parties can exit fairly if the relationship is no longer working.
Brand Partnership Contract
Ambassador & Long-Term Sponsorship Agreement

This Brand Partnership Contract (this "Agreement") is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] by and between [BRAND LEGAL NAME] ("Brand") and [CREATOR FULL LEGAL NAME], known online as [HANDLE / CHANNEL NAME] ("Ambassador"). This Agreement sets forth the terms of an ongoing brand ambassador relationship for the period specified below.

1. Partnership Term

The initial term of this partnership shall commence on [START DATE] and continue through [END DATE] (the "Initial Term"). Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [e.g., 30] days prior to the end of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive [e.g., 3-month] periods under the same terms and conditions, unless renegotiated by mutual written agreement.

2. Scope of Partnership

Ambassador agrees to serve as an official brand ambassador for Brand, promoting [BRAND NAME / PRODUCT LINE] authentically and enthusiastically across Ambassador's platforms including [LIST PLATFORMS, e.g., Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, newsletter]. Ambassador shall represent Brand in a manner consistent with Brand's guidelines as provided in writing and Ambassador's authentic personal brand. Ambassador shall not take any action that disparages Brand, its products, or its team during the Term of this Agreement.

3. Content Calendar & Deliverables

The Parties agree to the following recurring content commitments per [month / quarter]:

  • [NUMBER] dedicated sponsored posts / videos per month on [PLATFORM(S)].
  • [NUMBER] Stories or short-form mentions per month.
  • [NUMBER] brand mentions integrated organically (not standalone sponsored posts) per month.
  • Attendance at [NUMBER, e.g., up to 2] Brand events per year (virtual or in-person), with expenses covered by Brand.
  • [ANY ADDITIONAL, e.g., quarterly newsletter feature, podcast mention].

The Parties shall collaborate on a rolling content calendar at the beginning of each [month / quarter]. Brand shall submit its content requests no later than [e.g., the 1st of each month]. Ambassador is not required to exceed the agreed deliverable counts without written agreement and additional compensation.

4. Content Approval Process

Ambassador shall submit a brief content outline or draft to Brand at least [e.g., 5] business days before the planned posting date. Brand shall respond with written approval or one round of feedback within [e.g., 48 hours]. If Brand does not respond within the review window, content is deemed approved. Brand shall not require Ambassador to make claims about Brand products that are false, misleading, or inconsistent with Ambassador's genuine experience. Ambassador retains the right to decline requests that conflict with Ambassador's personal values or audience trust, provided Ambassador gives Brand written notice and a reasonable opportunity to modify the request.

5. Compensation & Payment Schedule

In consideration for the services herein, Brand shall pay Ambassador:

  • Base Retainer: [$AMOUNT] per [month / quarter], payable by the [e.g., 15th] of each [month / quarter].
  • Product Allowance: Brand shall provide Ambassador with [e.g., $X worth of product per month / N/A] at no charge.
  • Affiliate Commission: Ambassador shall receive [e.g., 10%] commission on net sales generated through Ambassador's unique promo code [CODE] or trackable affiliate link, paid within [e.g., 30] days after each calendar month.

Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of [e.g., 1.5%] per month on the outstanding balance.

6. Performance Bonuses

Ambassador shall be eligible for performance bonuses as follows:

  • If Ambassador's content generates [e.g., $X in attributed sales / X number of conversions / X% engagement rate] in a given [month / quarter], Brand shall pay a bonus of [$BONUS AMOUNT or X% of excess sales].
  • [ADD ADDITIONAL BONUS TIERS AS APPLICABLE].

Brand shall provide Ambassador with transparent reporting on attributed sales and conversions on a [monthly / quarterly] basis.

7. Exclusivity Windows

During the Term, Ambassador agrees not to enter into paid brand partnerships or ambassador agreements with direct competitors of Brand. "Direct competitor" is defined as any brand offering [SPECIFIC PRODUCT/SERVICE CATEGORY] to consumers. This exclusivity does not restrict Ambassador from: (a) discussing or reviewing non-sponsored competitive products; (b) partnerships in entirely unrelated categories; or (c) any existing partnerships disclosed in Exhibit A attached hereto. In recognition of this exclusivity, Brand agrees that the base retainer as set forth in Section 5 reflects an exclusivity premium. Ambassador shall disclose any pre-existing partnerships to Brand, and Brand acknowledges that those disclosed partnerships are exempt from this exclusivity obligation.

8. Intellectual Property Ownership

Ambassador retains full ownership of all content, imagery, video, and other creative work produced under this Agreement (collectively, "Ambassador Content"). Brand is granted a non-exclusive, worldwide license to repost, share, and display Ambassador Content on Brand's owned social media channels and website throughout the Term and for [e.g., 6 months] following its expiration. Any use of Ambassador Content for paid advertising, media buys, or whitelisting requires a separate written agreement and additional license fee. Brand shall always credit Ambassador when sharing Ambassador Content.

9. Morality Clause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) is charged with or convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude; (b) makes public statements or takes actions that a reasonable person would find to be hateful, discriminatory, or deeply offensive; (c) engages in conduct that brings significant public disrepute, scandal, or ridicule upon the other Party; or (d) materially misrepresents facts publicly in a manner that damages the other Party's reputation. Brand acknowledges that this clause applies reciprocally — conduct by Brand that harms Ambassador's reputation gives Ambassador the right to exit without penalty under this Section.

10. Exit Clause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement without cause by providing [e.g., 30] days' written notice to the other Party. Upon termination without cause: (a) Brand shall pay all amounts owed for completed and approved Deliverables through the termination date; (b) Brand shall pay a pro-rata portion of the monthly retainer for the notice period; (c) any ongoing exclusivity obligations shall terminate simultaneously with the Agreement; and (d) previously licensed content may remain published for the remainder of the agreed license period unless otherwise negotiated. Neither Party shall be liable for punitive damages upon a good-faith exit under this clause.

11. FTC & Regulatory Compliance

Ambassador shall comply with all applicable FTC guidelines regarding sponsored content disclosure. All sponsored Deliverables shall include conspicuous disclosures such as #ad, #sponsored, or equivalent platform-native disclosure tools. Ambassador shall not make false or unsubstantiated claims about Brand's products. Brand shall provide Ambassador with accurate product information and any required substantiation for product claims. Both Parties agree to cooperate promptly if contacted by any regulatory authority regarding this partnership.

12. Representations & Warranties

Each Party represents and warrants that: (a) it has full authority to enter into and perform this Agreement; (b) its performance will not violate any third-party rights or applicable law; and (c) all information provided to the other Party is accurate and complete to the best of its knowledge.

13. Limitation of Liability

In no event shall either Party be liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, consequential, punitive, or special damages arising from or related to this Agreement, even if advised of the possibility of such damages. Each Party's total aggregate liability to the other shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable under this Agreement in the [e.g., 3] months immediately preceding the claim.

14. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [STATE]. Any disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in [CITY, STATE] as described in any applicable arbitration addendum, or by the courts of [COUNTY, STATE] if arbitration is waived in writing by both Parties.

15. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with any attached Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning its subject matter. It may only be amended by a written document signed by both Parties. Waiver of any provision shall not constitute a waiver of any other provision. This Agreement may be executed electronically, and such signatures shall be deemed legally binding.

Collaboration Agreement

Joint Creator Project Agreement

How to Use This Template

  • Use this contract whenever two or more creators are working together on a joint project — course, video series, podcast, digital product, event, etc.
  • The revenue split and IP ownership sections are the most critical — be explicit and get it in writing before you create anything together.
  • Define "revenue" precisely: gross vs. net, before or after platform fees and payment processing costs.
  • Credit requirements matter for SEO and attribution — specify exactly how each creator must be credited on all platforms.
  • Think through what happens if one party wants to exit or stops contributing — address this upfront to protect both parties.
  • If the project involves significant investment or potential revenue, consult an attorney before signing.
Collaboration Agreement
Joint Creator Project & Revenue Sharing Agreement

This Collaboration Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] by and between:

Creator A: [FULL LEGAL NAME], known online as [HANDLE / BRAND NAME] ("Creator A"); and

Creator B: [FULL LEGAL NAME], known online as [HANDLE / BRAND NAME] ("Creator B").

Creator A and Creator B are referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

1. Project Description

The Parties agree to collaborate on the following joint project (the "Project"): [DESCRIBE THE PROJECT IN DETAIL, e.g., a co-hosted 6-episode YouTube mini-series titled "X," a joint digital course titled "Y," a co-authored e-book titled "Z," etc.]. The Project is intended for publication / launch on [DATE] via [PLATFORM(S) / DISTRIBUTION CHANNEL(S)]. The estimated total project budget is [$AMOUNT / "to be borne by each Party equally" / "N/A"].

2. Roles & Responsibilities

Each Party's specific contributions to the Project are as follows:

Creator A shall be responsible for:

  • [e.g., Writing and recording all video scripts]
  • [e.g., Managing the project timeline and content calendar]
  • [e.g., Primary promotion on Creator A's channels]
  • [ANY ADDITIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES]

Creator B shall be responsible for:

  • [e.g., Video editing and post-production]
  • [e.g., Thumbnail design and visual branding]
  • [e.g., Primary promotion on Creator B's channels]
  • [ANY ADDITIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES]

Each Party agrees to complete their responsibilities in good faith, on time, and at a professional standard. If a Party is unable to fulfill a responsibility, they shall notify the other Party in writing as soon as reasonably practicable so alternatives can be arranged.

3. Revenue Split

All net revenue generated from the Project shall be divided as follows:

  • Creator A: [e.g., 50%]
  • Creator B: [e.g., 50%]

For purposes of this Agreement, "net revenue" means all gross revenue received from the Project minus: (a) applicable platform fees and payment processing fees; (b) any agreed-upon project expenses documented in writing and pre-approved by both Parties. Revenue distributions shall be made within [e.g., 15] days after the end of each [month / quarter] in which revenue is received. The Party receiving or collecting revenue on behalf of the Project shall provide the other Party with a written accounting statement with each distribution. Neither Party shall deduct management fees, agent commissions, or personal overhead without the express written consent of the other Party.

4. Expenses

All Project-related expenses exceeding [$THRESHOLD, e.g., $100] individually must be pre-approved in writing by both Parties. Each Party is responsible for their own personal equipment, software, and overhead unless otherwise agreed. Approved shared expenses shall be deducted from gross revenue before the split described in Section 3 is applied. Each Party shall maintain records of all Project expenses and provide receipts upon request.

5. Content Ownership

All content created specifically for the Project and jointly by both Parties (the "Joint Work") shall be jointly owned by both Parties in equal undivided shares, unless otherwise specified in a separate written addendum. Each Party's pre-existing intellectual property (including their personal brand, logo, prior content, and proprietary methods) remains solely owned by that Party. Neither Party may license the Joint Work to third parties for commercial purposes without the written consent of the other Party. In the event the Parties wish to create derivative works from the Joint Work, they shall negotiate terms in good faith at that time.

6. Approval Rights

Neither Party shall publish, post, or distribute any Project content without the written approval of the other Party. Each Party shall have [e.g., 48 hours] to review and approve submitted drafts. If no response is given within the review window, the content shall be deemed approved. Each Party has the right to request revisions to ensure the Project content accurately represents their views and maintains their professional standards. Approval shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed.

7. Credit Requirements

All Project content shall credit both Parties equally and prominently. Specifically:

  • All titles, thumbnails, and cover art shall feature both Parties' names / handles with equal visual prominence.
  • All written descriptions, captions, and metadata shall list both Parties as co-creators.
  • Any press coverage, podcast appearances, or interviews discussing the Project shall reference both Parties as co-creators.
  • Neither Party shall represent the Project as solely their own work.

8. Exclusivity During Project

The Parties [do / do not] agree to exclusivity during the Project. If applicable: Neither Party shall collaborate with a direct competitor on a substantially similar project during the period from [START DATE] to [END DATE] without providing written notice to the other Party.

9. Term & Termination

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until the Project is complete and all revenue obligations are fulfilled, unless terminated earlier. Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing [e.g., 14] days' written notice. Upon termination: (a) all completed Joint Work shall remain jointly owned; (b) revenue obligations on existing published content shall survive termination and continue to be split per Section 3; (c) the Parties shall negotiate in good faith whether any partially completed content shall be finished, shelved, or released in its current form; and (d) each Party may retain their own pre-existing intellectual property and any content created solely by them.

10. Dispute Resolution

The Parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute arising from this Agreement through good-faith discussion first. If a dispute cannot be resolved within [e.g., 21] days, the Parties agree to attempt mediation through a mutually agreed-upon mediator before pursuing arbitration or litigation. Any legal action shall be governed by the laws of [STATE] and brought in the courts of [COUNTY, STATE].

11. Non-Disparagement

During the Term and for [e.g., 12 months] following its expiration or termination, neither Party shall make public statements that disparage, defame, or materially damage the reputation of the other Party, the other Party's brand, or the Project itself.

12. Entire Agreement

This Agreement represents the complete understanding of the Parties regarding the Project and supersedes all prior discussions. It may only be modified in a signed writing. Electronic signatures are valid and binding.

Non-Disclosure Agreement

Mutual NDA for Business Discussions

How to Use This Template

  • Use this NDA before sharing sensitive business information in pitch meetings, partnership discussions, or joint venture negotiations.
  • This is a mutual (bilateral) NDA — both parties are bound by the same obligations. For one-way confidentiality (e.g., you're protecting only your info), remove the mutual language.
  • Standard NDA terms run 2–3 years. Longer terms are harder to enforce. Trade secrets may have indefinite protection under applicable law regardless of contract term.
  • The exclusions section (Section 4) is important and fair — don't try to remove it. Information that becomes public through no fault of the receiving party shouldn't remain confidential.
  • NDAs don't prevent all information leakage — they just create legal recourse if someone breaches. Use them alongside good judgment about what you share.
Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement
Confidential Business Information Protection Agreement

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] (the "Effective Date") by and between:

Party 1: [FULL LEGAL NAME / ENTITY NAME], [an individual residing at / a ENTITY TYPE with its principal place of business at] [ADDRESS] ("Party 1"); and

Party 2: [FULL LEGAL NAME / ENTITY NAME], [an individual residing at / a ENTITY TYPE with its principal place of business at] [ADDRESS] ("Party 2").

Party 1 and Party 2 are individually referred to as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties." This Agreement is entered into in connection with the Parties' discussions regarding [DESCRIBE PURPOSE, e.g., a potential business partnership / content collaboration / investment / joint venture] (the "Purpose").

1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether disclosed orally, in writing, digitally, visually, or by any other means, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, strategies, financial projections and data, revenue figures, audience analytics, subscriber and follower data, unreleased product or content concepts, partnerships in negotiation, pricing structures, proprietary processes and methodologies, client and vendor lists, trade secrets, technical data, marketing strategies, and personal information about the Disclosing Party's team or associates.

2. Obligations of Confidentiality

Each Receiving Party agrees to:

  • Hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence.
  • Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described above and for no other purpose.
  • Not disclose, share, transmit, publish, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.
  • Limit internal access to the Confidential Information to employees, contractors, advisors, and representatives who have a need to know such information in furtherance of the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.
  • Take reasonable precautions — at least those the Receiving Party uses to protect its own confidential information of similar sensitivity, and no less than reasonable care — to safeguard the Confidential Information against unauthorized access, disclosure, or use.
  • Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information.

3. Permitted Disclosures

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information without consent to the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided that: (a) the Receiving Party provides the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice (to the extent legally permissible) so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; (b) the Receiving Party cooperates with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to obtain such a protective order; and (c) the Receiving Party discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information legally required to be disclosed.

4. Exclusions from Confidential Information

The obligations of this Agreement shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by documentary evidence:

  • Is or becomes publicly available through no act or omission of the Receiving Party;
  • Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party and without any confidentiality obligation;
  • Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;
  • Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
  • Has been approved for release in writing by the Disclosing Party.

5. Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of [e.g., two (2) / three (3)] years from the Effective Date (the "Term"). Notwithstanding the foregoing, obligations with respect to any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret, regardless of the expiration of the Term. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon [e.g., 30] days' written notice, but all obligations of confidentiality with respect to Confidential Information received prior to termination shall survive for the remainder of the Term.

6. Return or Destruction of Materials

Upon the Disclosing Party's written request, or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly: (a) return to the Disclosing Party all tangible materials containing or embodying Confidential Information; and (b) permanently destroy or delete all digital copies, notes, summaries, and derivatives of the Confidential Information and certify in writing that such destruction has been completed. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy of Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law or regulation, subject to the continuing confidentiality obligations herein.

7. No License or Rights Granted

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting either Party any license, right, or interest in any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or any other intellectual property right of the other Party. The disclosure of Confidential Information under this Agreement does not commit either Party to entering into any further agreement, partnership, or business relationship.

8. No Warranty
